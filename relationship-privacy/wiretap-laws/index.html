✓ One-Party Consent 36 States

You can record if YOU are part of the conversation (no need to inform the other party).

⚠ Two-Party Consent 14 States

ALL parties must consent to being recorded. Recording without consent is a crime.

📍 Interstate Recording Rules

When recording across state lines, the stricter state's law generally applies. If you're in a one-party state calling someone in a two-party state (like California), you likely need their consent. Federal wiretap law (18 U.S.C. § 2511) requires at least one-party consent and can impose criminal penalties up to 5 years imprisonment.
