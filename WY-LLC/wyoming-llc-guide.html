üõ°Ô∏è What is Charging Order Protection?
‚ñº

Charging order protection is Wyoming's most powerful asset protection feature. If a creditor sues you personally and wins a judgment:

  • In most states, they can seize your LLC ownership and force liquidation
  • In Wyoming, they can only get a "charging order"‚Äîthe right to receive distributions if the LLC makes any
  • The creditor cannot force distributions, vote, manage, or access LLC assets
  • You (as manager) can simply choose not to distribute anything, leaving the creditor with nothing
  • Even better: The creditor may owe phantom income tax on undistributed profits (often forcing settlement)

Wyoming's advantage: This protection applies to single-member LLCs (many states don't protect single-member LLCs). Makes Wyoming ideal for holding real estate, investments, or operating assets.

üè† Can I Use Wyoming LLC for Real Estate?
‚ñº

Yes, and it's very common. Real estate investors often use Wyoming LLCs to:

  • Privacy: Property ownership is public record, but if the LLC owns it, only the LLC name appears (not your personal name)
  • Asset Protection: Each property in a separate Wyoming LLC isolates liability (one lawsuit can't reach other properties)
  • Estate Planning: Easier to transfer LLC interests to heirs than deeding individual properties

Important: If the property is located in another state (e.g., California rental), you must also register as a foreign LLC in that state. Wyoming LLC provides privacy and protection, but you still comply with local landlord-tenant laws and pay property taxes.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

‚ùì Do I need to visit Wyoming to form an LLC?
‚ñº

No. You can form a Wyoming LLC entirely remotely. You don't need to visit Wyoming, have a physical office there, or even set foot in the state. You just need a registered agent with a Wyoming address (which we provide).

‚ùì Can non-U.S. residents form a Wyoming LLC?
‚ñº

Yes. Wyoming allows foreign nationals to form and own LLCs. You don't need U.S. citizenship or residency. However, you will need:

  • A U.S. mailing address (can be your registered agent's address)
  • EIN from IRS (we can help with this)
  • Compliance with FATCA and tax treaty obligations (consult tax advisor)

Many international investors use Wyoming LLCs to hold U.S. real estate or investments.

‚ùì What if I already have an LLC in another state?
‚ñº

You have options:

  • Keep both: Operate existing LLC in original state for active business; use Wyoming LLC for holding assets, IP, or investments
  • Domesticate: Some states allow you to "move" your LLC to Wyoming (called domestication or conversion). Not all states permit this.
  • Wind down old LLC: Dissolve existing LLC and transfer assets to new Wyoming LLC

I can advise on the best approach for your situation. Email me with details about your current LLC.

‚ùì How do I pay Wyoming LLC taxes?
‚ñº

Wyoming has NO state income tax. You don't pay Wyoming taxes on LLC profits. However:

  • Federal taxes: LLC income flows through to your personal tax return (or you can elect S-corp or C-corp taxation)
  • Other state taxes: If you operate in another state, you may owe taxes there based on nexus rules
  • Sales tax: If you sell taxable goods/services in Wyoming, you collect 4% state sales tax (plus local taxes)

Recommendation: Consult a CPA familiar with multi-state taxation to optimize your tax structure.

‚ùì Is Wyoming LLC good for cryptocurrency/digital assets?
‚ñº

Yes, Wyoming is crypto-friendly. Wyoming passed several blockchain and digital asset laws, including:

  • Clear legal status: Digital assets are recognized as property
  • DAO LLCs: Wyoming allows Decentralized Autonomous Organization LLCs
  • No state capital gains tax: 0% on crypto gains (federal still applies)
  • Asset protection: Crypto held in Wyoming LLC gets same charging order protection as other assets

Many crypto investors use Wyoming LLCs to hold Bitcoin, Ethereum, NFTs, and other digital assets with privacy and protection.

‚ùì What's the Corporate Transparency Act (CTA) impact?
‚ñº

As of 2024, most LLCs must report beneficial ownership information to FinCEN (Financial Crimes Enforcement Network):

  • Who reports: LLCs formed after Jan 1, 2024 (existing LLCs have until 2025)
  • What's reported: Names, addresses, and ID of all beneficial owners (25%+ ownership)
  • Where it goes: Federal database (not public, but accessible to law enforcement and banks)
  • Wyoming impact: CTA is federal law‚Äîaffects all U.S. LLCs regardless of state

Privacy note: Wyoming still doesn't publicly disclose owners (state records), but you must report to FinCEN. We can assist with CTA compliance filings.
