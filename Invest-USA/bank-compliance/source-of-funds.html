SOF vs SOW: What's the Difference?

‚ñº

Source of Funds (SOF)

  • Where this specific money came from
  • Required for most wire transfers
  • Example: "Proceeds from apartment sale"

Source of Wealth (SOW)

  • How you accumulated overall wealth
  • Required for transfers over $500K
  • Example: "20 years as tech entrepreneur"

üíº Employment Income / Salary Savings

‚ñº

Easiest source to document with clear paper trail.

Required Documents

  • Employment letter (name, title, salary, dates)
  • Pay stubs (3-6 months)
  • Tax returns (2-3 years)
  • Bank statements (12-24 months)
  • Employment contract

What Banks Verify

  • Consistent salary deposits
  • Legitimate employer
  • Income matches accumulated savings
  • Tax returns show declared income

üè¢ Business Income / Self-Employment

‚ñº

Requires more documentation than employment income.

Required Documents

  • Business registration documents
  • Business tax returns (2-3 years)
  • Financial statements (audited if available)
  • Business bank statements (12-24 months)
  • Personal tax returns
  • Dividend/distribution docs

What Banks Verify

  • Legitimate operating business
  • Proper registration/licensing
  • Financial statements show profitability
  • Clear trail: business ‚Üí personal accounts

üè† Real Estate Sale Proceeds

‚ñº

Most common source for foreign investors.

Required Documents

  • Property deed/title (prior ownership)
  • Sale contract (signed)
  • Closing statement
  • Wire/check showing receipt
  • Property appraisal
  • Mortgage payoff statement

What Banks Verify

  • You owned the property
  • Arm's length sale
  • Clear receipt of proceeds
  • Reasonable sale price

üí∞ Business Sale Proceeds

‚ñº

Common for E-2 or EB-5 investors.

Required Documents

  • Purchase and sale agreement
  • Closing documents
  • Proof of prior ownership
  • Bank statements showing receipt
  • Capital gains tax documents

What Banks Verify

  • Documented sale with clear terms
  • You owned the business
  • Payment was received
  • Proper tax treatment

üìà Securities / Brokerage Proceeds

‚ñº

Required Documents

  • Brokerage statements (12-24 months)
  • Trade confirmations
  • Tax forms (1099-B or equivalent)
  • Wire/ACH from brokerage
  • Bank statements showing receipt

What Banks Verify

  • Legitimate brokerage account
  • History of investment activity
  • Clear sale transactions
  • Trail from brokerage to bank

‚Çø Cryptocurrency Proceeds

‚ñº
‚ö†Ô∏è High Scrutiny ‚Äî Some banks refuse crypto proceeds entirely

Convert through major regulated exchange, season funds 3-6 months before wire.

Required Documents

  • Exchange statements (purchase history)
  • Sale/conversion from regulated exchange
  • Wire from exchange to bank

  • Mixer/tumbler services
  • Unregulated exchanges
  • No tax reporting

üìú Inheritance

‚ñº

Required Documents

  • Death certificate
  • Will naming you as beneficiary
  • Probate/estate documents
  • Letter from estate executor

What Banks Verify

  • Legal inheritance documentation

üéÅ Gift from Family

‚ñº
‚ö° Enhanced Scrutiny ‚Äî Donor must also prove source of wealth

Required Documents

  • Gift letter (signed, dated)
  • Donor's bank statements
  • Donor's source of wealth docs
  • Relationship proof
  • Gift tax return (if applicable)

Gift Letter Must Include

  • Donor's name, address, relationship
  • Dollar amount and date
  • "No repayment expected" statement
  • Source of the gifted funds
  • Donor's signature
