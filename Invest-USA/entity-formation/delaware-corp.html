Why Choose a Delaware C-Corporation

A Delaware C-Corporation is the default structure for venture-backed startups. Over 80% of US companies that go public are Delaware corporations. If you are building a company to raise institutional capital, this is the structure investors expect.

Venture Capital Ready

VCs have standard term sheets designed for Delaware C-Corps. Using a different structure creates friction and may cause investors to pass.

Stock Options

C-Corps can issue incentive stock options (ISOs) with favorable tax treatment for employees. Essential for attracting US talent.

Clear Governance

Board of Directors, officers, and shareholder rights are well-defined. No ambiguity about who controls the company.

IPO Ready

When you go public, you will not need to restructure. Delaware corporations are the standard for NYSE and NASDAQ listings.

Court of Chancery

Delaware's specialized business court provides expert judges and predictable outcomes for corporate disputes.

Established Precedent

Decades of case law mean attorneys know exactly how Delaware courts interpret corporate documents.

Double Taxation Warning

C-Corporations pay corporate income tax on profits, and shareholders pay personal tax on dividends. This "double taxation" is the trade-off for the benefits above. If you are not raising VC or planning an IPO, consider an LLC instead.

Typical Stock Structure for Startups

When incorporating, you need to decide on your authorized share structure. Here is a typical setup for a VC-backed startup:

Share Class Typical Authorization Purpose
Common Stock 10,000,000 shares Founders, employees, option pool
Preferred Stock 5,000,000 shares Reserved for investor rounds

Par Value Tip

Set your par value at $0.00001 or $0.0001. This minimizes Delaware franchise tax and is standard practice. Never set par value at $1.00 unless you have a specific reason.

Delaware Corporation Costs

  • Formation Fee: $89 (state filing fee)
  • Annual Franchise Tax: Minimum $225 (increases with shares/assets)
  • Registered Agent: $50-150/year (required)
  • Annual Report: $50/year

For startups with significant authorized shares, use the Assumed Par Value (APV) method to calculate franchise tax. This often results in the minimum $225 payment rather than thousands based on share count.

Formation Process

  1. Choose your corporation name - Must be unique in Delaware and include "Inc.", "Corp.", or "Corporation"
  2. Prepare Articles of Incorporation - Use our generator or work with an attorney
  3. Appoint a Registered Agent - Must have a physical Delaware address
  4. File with Delaware - Submit Certificate of Incorporation ($89 fee)
  5. Draft Bylaws - Internal governance document (not filed with state)
  6. Hold Organizational Meeting - Adopt bylaws, elect directors, authorize share issuance
  7. Issue Founder Shares - Document with stock purchase agreements and 83(b) elections
  8. Obtain EIN - Apply for Employer Identification Number from IRS
