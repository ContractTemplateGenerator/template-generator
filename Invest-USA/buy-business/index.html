E-2 Visa Through Business Acquisition

â–¼

If you are from a treaty country, buying a US business can qualify you for an E-2 investor visa.

Requirements

  • Treaty country nationality
  • Substantial investment ($100K+ typical)
  • Active management role
  • Business not marginal
  • Investment at risk

Benefits

  • Live and work in US
  • Spouse can work (EAD)
  • Children can study
  • Renewable indefinitely
  • No annual caps
I work with immigration attorneys to structure acquisitions that maximize E-2 approval chances

Special Considerations for Foreign Buyers

â–¼

Financing

  • Most SBA loans require US credit
  • Seller financing common (30-50%)
  • All-cash or international bank
  • Prepare substantial down payment

Entity Structure

  • Form LLC/Corp 30-60 days before close
  • Obtain EIN
  • Open US bank account early
  • Shows commitment to seller

Visa Timing

  • Coordinate with immigration attorney
  • May close before visa application
  • Process takes 2-6 months
  • Consider contingencies

Wire Transfers

  • Source-of-funds documentation
  • Coordinate with banks both ends
  • Avoid compliance holds at closing
  • Plan transfer timing carefully
