Why Foreign Investors Choose Nevada

Nevada has become a magnet for international real estate investors, driven by tax advantages, strong population growth, and the entertainment economy. When I advise foreign clients on Nevada investments, I highlight these key benefits:

  • No state income tax: Zero tax on rental income, capital gains, or personal income
  • Strong asset protection: Nevada LLC laws are among the strongest in the nation
  • Rapid population growth: Las Vegas metro has been among the fastest-growing US cities
  • Tourism economy: 40+ million visitors annually support short-term rental demand
  • Affordable compared to California: Homes cost 40-60% less than comparable LA/SF properties
  • No state inheritance tax: Better estate planning outcomes for foreign investors

The California Exodus Effect

I have helped many clients purchase Nevada properties specifically to benefit from the ongoing migration from California. Las Vegas is just 4 hours from Los Angeles, and Reno is even closer to the Bay Area. This migration has driven strong appreciation and rental demand.

What You Will Pay

  • Property taxes: Approximately 0.5-1% of assessed value (lower than national average)
  • Transfer tax: $2.55-$2.90 per $500 of value (paid by seller)
  • Federal taxes: Still apply - FIRPTA withholding on sale, federal income tax on rental income
  • Commerce Tax: Only for businesses with gross revenue over $4 million

California Comparison

A California investor with $100,000 in rental income would pay approximately $9,300 in state taxes. A Nevada investor pays $0. Over 10 years, that is nearly $100,000 in savings - before considering capital gains on sale. This is why I often recommend my California clients consider Nevada properties.

Short-Term Rental Regulations

Many of my foreign clients are attracted to Las Vegas for short-term rental income. Here is what you need to know:

Clark County (Unincorporated Areas)

  • Short-term rentals (under 31 days) require a license
  • Must be owner-occupied or have a local responsible party
  • 13% transient lodging tax applies
  • Annual renewal and inspections required

City of Las Vegas

  • Vacation rentals require a Special Use Permit in most areas
  • Distance requirements from other vacation rentals
  • More restrictive than unincorporated Clark County

Henderson

  • Generally prohibits short-term rentals in residential areas
  • Some exceptions for certain communities
  • Focus on long-term rental or personal use instead

HOA Restrictions Often Stricter Than Law

Even where local law permits short-term rentals, many HOAs prohibit or restrict them. I always advise my clients to review CC&Rs carefully before purchasing for short-term rental purposes. Some communities have banned Airbnb-style rentals entirely.

Nevada LLC for Real Estate

Nevada is famous for strong LLC protections, and I often structure my clients' real estate holdings in Nevada LLCs:

Key Benefits of Nevada LLCs

  • Charging order protection: Creditors cannot seize LLC assets, only distributions
  • Single-member protection: Unlike many states, single-member LLCs get full protection
  • Privacy: Member names not required in public filings (as of formation)
  • No state income tax: LLC income flows through without state taxation
  • Series LLC available: Separate assets into protected cells within one LLC

Nevada LLC for Out-of-State Property?

Some clients ask about using Nevada LLCs for property in other states. While possible, the property's location determines taxation and some legal protections. A Nevada LLC holding California property still pays California taxes. I help clients determine the optimal structure for their specific situation.

The Purchase Process

Nevada has a straightforward purchase process that I guide my clients through:

  1. Property search: Work with a buyer's agent familiar with foreign buyers
  2. Offer and negotiation: Standard purchase agreement, typically 1-2% earnest money
  3. Due diligence period: Usually 10-15 days for inspections
  4. Escrow and title: Title company handles closing
  5. Closing: Can be done remotely with proper documentation

Total timeline: 30-45 days for a typical cash transaction.

FIRPTA Considerations

Even with Nevada's zero state tax, federal FIRPTA rules apply when you sell:

  • 15% of gross sale price withheld by buyer
  • Withholding certificate can reduce or eliminate withholding if tax liability is lower
  • File US tax return to claim refund of excess withholding
