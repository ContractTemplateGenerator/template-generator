Requirements

  • Proof of address (utility bill)

Process

  • Get tax ID at tax office (same day)
  • Visit bank branch in person
  • KYC interview and documentation
  • Account active in 1-3 days

Path to US Investment

Turkey to USA Investment Flow

â–¼

Typical path for using Turkey as transit to US investment:

  1. Establish Turkish residence (rental or property)
  2. Open Turkish bank account with proper KYC
  3. Transfer funds to Turkey (from origin country)
  4. Establish track record in Turkey (6+ months recommended)
  5. Open US brokerage account (Interactive Brokers accepts Turkey)
  6. SWIFT transfer from Turkey to US broker
  7. Maintain documentation of entire flow

E-2 Visa via Turkish Citizenship

â–¼

Turkey is an E-2 treaty country. This provides a path for nationals of non-treaty countries:

The Path

  • Obtain Turkish citizenship ($400K+ property)
  • Apply for E-2 as Turkish national
  • Invest in US business ($100K+)
  • Live and work in USA

Timeline

  • Turkish citizenship: 3-6 months
  • E-2 visa application: 2-4 months
  • Total: 6-12 months
  • Faster than most alternatives
This path is particularly valuable for Russian nationals who otherwise have no E-2 treaty access.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is using Turkey for transit legal?

â–¼

Yes, if done properly. Having genuine presence in Turkey, opening compliant bank accounts, and maintaining proper documentation is legal. What is illegal is using Turkey solely to evade sanctions or circumvent reporting requirements. The key is genuine economic activity, not shell arrangements.

Will US banks accept transfers from Turkey?

â–¼

Generally yes, but with enhanced scrutiny. Turkey is not sanctioned. However, compliance departments know Turkey is used as a transit hub. Expect questions about source of funds, purpose of transfer, and your connection to Turkey. Proper documentation makes this manageable.

How long should I stay in Turkey before transferring?

â–¼

There is no legal minimum, but practical advice: establish presence for at least 3-6 months before large transfers. This creates a genuine paper trail. Arriving, depositing funds, and immediately transferring out looks like transit/evasion and may trigger blocks.

What about Turkish citizenship for E-2?

â–¼

This is a legitimate strategy. Purchase $400K+ property, obtain Turkish citizenship (3-6 months), then apply for E-2 visa as a Turkish national. Total investment: ~$500K ($400K property + $100K US business). Total time: 6-12 months. Faster and cheaper than EB-5.

Is Turkey better than Kazakhstan/Armenia for transit?

â–¼

Depends on your situation. Kazakhstan is easier for Russians (language, IIN process). Armenia has simpler banking for small amounts. Turkey offers citizenship path and E-2 eligibility but has more compliance scrutiny. Choose based on your specific needs and long-term goals.
