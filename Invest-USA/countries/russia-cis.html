CIS Country Routing for Russian Investors

Kazakhstan, Armenia, and Georgia as compliant pathways to US investment

This guide is for non-sanctioned Russian nationals seeking legal capital mobility. Always verify sanctions status and work with qualified counsel.

The Transit Hub Concept

â–¼

Since major Russian banks are disconnected from SWIFT and cannot process USD transactions directly with Western banks, Russian nationals need an intermediate step. CIS countries offer this because:

Why These Countries

  • Maintain banking with both Russia and West
  • Accept Russian nationals for accounts
  • Connected to SWIFT network
  • No sanctions on their banking system

Legal Basis

  • Using third-country banking is legal
  • Must have genuine presence/residence
  • Must declare all income properly
  • Cannot structure to evade sanctions
Shell arrangements without genuine presence are sanctions evasion and are illegal.

Kazakhstan

Banking in Kazakhstan

â–¼

Kazakhstan has become the most popular transit hub for Russian capital due to its developed banking system and relative ease of account opening:

Key Banks

  • Kaspi Bank (easiest, mobile-first)
  • Halyk Bank (largest, international)
  • Forte Bank (good for business)
  • Freedom Finance Bank

Requirements

  • IIN (Individual ID Number)
  • Passport + local SIM card
  • Physical presence for IIN
  • Address registration (temporary OK)

Getting an IIN (Individual ID Number)

â–¼

The IIN is required for banking and is obtained at a Public Service Center (CON):

  1. Arrive in Kazakhstan (visa-free for Russians, 90 days)
  2. Get temporary registration at hotel or rental
  3. Visit CON with passport and registration
  4. Receive IIN same day or next day
  5. Use IIN to open bank account
Kaspi Bank allows account opening via app once you have IIN, no branch visit needed.

Armenia

Banking in Armenia

â–¼

Armenia offers visa-free entry for Russians and has maintained banking relationships with both Russia and Western correspondent banks:

Key Banks

  • Ameriabank (largest, international)
  • Ardshinbank (good for transfers)
  • Converse Bank
  • ACBA Bank

Account Opening

  • Passport required
  • Proof of address (rental contract)
  • Source of funds documentation
  • Branch visit required

Residence Options

â–¼

Armenia offers several paths to legal residence:

Temporary Residence (1 year)

  • Work permit path
  • Business/investment path
  • Study path
  • Renewable annually

Benefits

  • Easier bank account approval
  • Higher transfer limits
  • Access to investment accounts
  • Tax treaty benefits
IT professionals can use Armenia's special IT visa program for faster residence.

Transfer Capabilities

â–¼

Inbound (from Russia)

  • RUB transfers via MIR system
  • Some Russian banks still connect
  • Koronapay transfers work
  • Unistream available

Outbound (to USA)

  • SWIFT transfers available
  • USD accounts possible
  • Correspondent banking intact
  • Reasonable fees

Georgia

Banking in Georgia

â–¼

Georgia has become more cautious about Russian accounts but remains an option, especially for those with EU/Western ties:

Key Banks

  • Bank of Georgia (largest)
  • TBC Bank (modern, digital)
  • Liberty Bank
  • Basis Bank

Current Challenges

  • Stricter KYC for Russians
  • Source of funds heavily scrutinized
  • Some banks declining Russians
  • Higher documentation requirements
Georgia has tightened requirements for Russian nationals. Prepare extensive documentation.

Visa-Free Stay

Entry Rules

  • No registration required
  • Can exit and re-enter
  • Work permit needed for employment

Residence Permit

  • Investment path ($100K+ property)
  • Employment path
  • Remote work program available
  • Freelancer/digital nomad options

Step-by-Step Process

Typical Path: Russia to US Investment

â–¼

A compliant process typically involves these steps:

  1. Sanctions Screening: Verify you are not on SDN or other lists
  2. Travel: Fly to chosen CIS country (Kazakhstan recommended)
  3. Registration: Obtain local ID (IIN in Kazakhstan)
  4. Bank Account: Open local bank account with proper KYC
  5. Transfer In: Move RUB from Russia to local account
  6. Convert: Exchange RUB to USD at local bank
  7. Transfer Out: SWIFT transfer USD to US bank/broker
  8. Document: Keep complete records of all transactions
Skip no steps. Each transaction should be documented and explainable.

Compliance Requirements

What Makes Routing Legal vs Illegal

â–¼

Legal (Compliant)

  • Genuine physical presence
  • Real local registration
  • Transparent banking relationship
  • Documented source of funds
  • Proper tax reporting

Illegal (Sanctions Evasion)

  • Shell accounts with no presence
  • Using nominees to hide identity
  • Structuring to avoid reporting
  • Misrepresenting source of funds
  • Acting for sanctioned persons

See our OFAC Sanctions Screening Guide for detailed compliance requirements.

Documentation to Maintain

â–¼

Personal Documents

  • Passport copies
  • Travel records (stamps, tickets)
  • Local registration documents
  • Rental/accommodation contracts

Financial Records

  • Bank statements (all countries)
  • Wire transfer confirmations
  • Currency exchange receipts
  • Tax filings (all jurisdictions)

Source of Funds

  • Employment records/salary slips
  • Business ownership documents
  • Investment records
  • Inheritance documentation
Keep records for minimum 7 years. Banks and regulators may request at any time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which country is best for me?

â–¼

Kazakhstan if you want the fastest, easiest bank account opening. Armenia if you want a clear residence path and IT-friendly environment. Georgia if you plan longer-term relocation and have non-Russian income sources or crypto. Each has trade-offs; the best choice depends on your specific situation.

Do I need to actually live there?

â–¼

You need genuine presence, not necessarily permanent residence. Opening an account remotely or using a nominee is sanctions evasion. A genuine trip, real registration, and in-person bank account opening is the minimum. Many people split time between Russia and the transit country.

What if the transit country gets sanctioned?

â–¼

This is a risk, particularly for Kazakhstan which has closer Russia ties. Monitor US Treasury/OFAC announcements. If sanctions are imposed, you may need to move funds elsewhere before cutoff dates. Having accounts in multiple jurisdictions provides flexibility.

How much can I transfer?

â–¼

Limits vary by bank and change frequently. Typical daily SWIFT limits are $50,000-$100,000. Larger amounts require additional documentation and may take longer. Never structure transactions to stay under limits - this is illegal. Work with your bank transparently on large transfers.

What about taxes?

â–¼

You may have tax obligations in Russia, the transit country, and the USA. Russia taxes worldwide income for residents. The transit country may tax income earned/received there. The USA taxes certain US-source income. Consult a cross-border tax specialist to structure properly.
