Frequently Asked Questions

Can I buy US real estate without a visa?
Yes. There are no citizenship or visa requirements to purchase US real estate. However, you'll need to comply with FIRPTA withholding rules when selling, and proper structuring (LLC vs. personal ownership) matters for liability and tax purposes.
What's the minimum investment for an E-2 visa?
There's no fixed minimum, but investments typically start at $100,000+ to be considered "substantial." The investment must be proportional to the total cost of the business. Franchises often work well for E-2 applications.
Can I open a US brokerage account from abroad?
Yes, several major brokerages accept foreign nationals. Interactive Brokers is the most popular choice. You'll need to complete a W-8BEN form for tax treaty benefits and understand dividend withholding rules.
Should I form an LLC in Delaware or Wyoming?
Delaware is best for businesses seeking investors or planning to go public. Wyoming offers better privacy and lower costs for smaller operations. For real estate, consider the state where the property is located.
How do I avoid wire transfer freezes?
Document the source of funds, use established banking relationships, provide business purpose documentation, and avoid round-number transfers. Consider using business accounts rather than personal accounts for large transfers.
What's the EB-5 investment requirement?
The standard investment is $1,050,000, reduced to $800,000 for investments in Targeted Employment Areas (TEAs) or rural areas. The investment must create at least 10 full-time jobs for US workers.
