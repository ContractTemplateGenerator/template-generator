Specialized NDAs for independent contractors, freelancers, and project-based work. Protect confidential information with proper IP assignment clauses.

Why Contractor NDAs Are Different

Unlike employee NDAs, contractor agreements must clearly define IP ownership, work-for-hire provisions, and the scope of confidential access. Without proper clauses, disputes over who owns the work product can be expensive and time-consuming.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a separate NDA and contractor agreement?
It depends on your situation. For simple projects, an NDA with IP assignment clauses may be sufficient. For complex engagements, you may want a full Independent Contractor Agreement that includes confidentiality provisions. Our templates are designed to work standalone or complement existing agreements.
What's the difference between work-for-hire and licensed IP?
Work-for-hire: The hiring party owns all IP from the start. The contractor has no rights to reuse or license the work.

Licensed IP: The contractor retains ownership but grants a license (exclusive or non-exclusive) to use the work. This allows contractors to maintain portfolio rights or reuse components.
How long should a contractor NDA last?
Most contractor NDAs have a confidentiality period of 2-5 years after the project ends. However, trade secrets should be protected indefinitely. Our templates allow you to set different terms for different types of information.
Can I use these for international contractors?
Yes, but you should specify the governing law (usually U.S. state law) and dispute resolution mechanism. Our templates include jurisdiction selection and can be adapted for international use. For significant international projects, consider consulting with an attorney familiar with the contractor's jurisdiction.
