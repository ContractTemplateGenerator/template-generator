What is a Relationship Privacy NDA?

A Relationship Privacy NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement) is a legally binding contract between romantic partners that establishes clear boundaries around what personal information can and cannot be shared with others. Unlike a standard NDA used in business contexts, a relationship privacy NDA is specifically designed to protect the intimate details, shared experiences, and personal vulnerabilities that emerge in romantic relationships.

This type of agreement addresses a modern reality: in an age of social media, screenshots, and digital permanence, private moments can become public instantly. A well-crafted relationship privacy NDA provides both parties with legal recourse if their privacy is violated, while also serving as a foundation for building trust through clear expectations.

What This NDA Protects

Personal Communications

All verbal conversations, text messages, emails, voice notes, video calls, and written correspondence between the parties. This includes both the content of communications and their existence.

Visual Media

Photographs, videos, and any visual recordings of either party, including intimate images, casual photos, and screenshots of conversations. The agreement establishes rules for retention and deletion of such materials.

Personal Information

  • Physical and mental health details
  • Financial information (income, debts, assets)
  • Family circumstances and relationships
  • Career and employment details
  • Living situation and address
  • Personal habits, preferences, and routines

Relationship Details

  • The existence or nature of the relationship
  • Details of activities and experiences shared together
  • Intimate or sexual information
  • Disagreements, conflicts, or difficulties
  • Plans for the future of the relationship
Important Limitation NDAs cannot be used to conceal illegal activity. If either party witnesses or is subject to domestic violence, abuse, harassment, or other crimes, they retain full legal rights to report such conduct to authorities. See our detailed guide on NDA limitations.
