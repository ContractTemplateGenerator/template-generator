Intimate Media NDA

Protect your intimate photos and videos with a specialized NDA. Designed to prevent revenge porn and non-consensual sharing of private visual content.

The Scope of the Problem

Non-consensual intimate image sharing (often called "revenge porn") is a growing crisis affecting millions of people. Understanding the scale of this problem helps illustrate why legal protection is essential.

An Intimate Media NDA provides an additional layer of protection beyond criminal laws. While revenge porn laws allow for prosecution after the fact, an NDA creates a civil remedy and serves as a powerful deterrent before any sharing occurs.

NDAs Provide Unique Benefits Unlike criminal prosecutions, NDA breaches can result in monetary damages, making recovery possible. NDAs also clearly establish expectations upfront, often preventing violations before they occur.

What This NDA Protects

Photographs

All intimate photos including nudes, semi-nudes, and suggestive images

Videos

Recorded video content of intimate moments or activities

Screenshots

Screenshots of video calls, messages, or other visual content

Live Streams

Private video calls and live streaming content

Cloud Storage

Content stored in cloud services, backup systems, or shared folders

Sexting Content

Intimate text messages containing visual content

Prohibited Actions

This NDA specifically prohibits:

  • Sharing: Sending intimate media to any other person
  • Posting: Publishing on any website, social media, or online platform
  • Selling: Monetizing intimate content in any way
  • Threatening: Using intimate media as leverage or for blackmail
  • Copying: Making additional copies for distribution
  • Showing: Displaying to others, even without making copies

Legal Landscape: State Laws

While 48 states now have laws against non-consensual intimate images, these laws vary significantly. An NDA provides consistent protection regardless of which state's laws might apply.

California (Civ. Code 1708.85)

Civil liability for intentional distribution. Damages include economic, non-economic, and punitive damages plus attorney fees.

New York (Civ. Rights Law 52-b)

Civil cause of action with damages up to $250,000. Also criminal penalties under Penal Law.

Texas (Civ. Prac. & Rem. 98B)

Civil liability with statutory damages. Also a Class A misdemeanor under Penal Code.

Florida (Fla. Stat. 784.049)

First-degree misdemeanor. Civil remedies include injunctions and actual damages.

Federal Law: SHIELD Act (Proposed) The SHIELD Act would create federal criminal penalties for non-consensual pornography. While not yet passed, this NDA will complement any future federal protections.

What To Do If This NDA Is Violated

If someone shares your intimate images in violation of this NDA, take immediate action:

  1. Document Everything: Take screenshots, save URLs, and preserve all evidence of the violation
  2. Do Not Contact the Violator: Any communication should go through an attorney
  3. Contact an Attorney: This NDA provides civil remedies - an attorney can help you enforce them
  4. Report to Platforms: Most major platforms have specific reporting mechanisms for intimate images
  5. Consider Criminal Charges: In most states, this is also a crime - contact local law enforcement
  6. Seek Removal Services: Organizations like CCRI and specialized attorneys can help remove content
