Manufacturing Industry

Manufacturing Trade Secrets

High Risk 3 versions available

💡 Plain English Explanation

Manufacturing trade secrets are the crown jewels of industrial enterprises. These include proprietary formulas, chemical compositions, production processes, and manufacturing techniques that give your company its competitive edge. Unlike patents, which require public disclosure, trade secrets can remain protected indefinitely as long as they stay confidential.

This clause specifically addresses the unique nature of manufacturing trade secrets, which often involve complex technical information that took years and significant R&D investment to develop. The clause must be comprehensive enough to cover everything from raw material specifications to final quality control procedures.

Key elements this clause addresses:

  • 1
    Formulas and compositions: Chemical formulas, material blends, ingredient ratios, and compound specifications that define product characteristics.
  • 2
    Manufacturing processes: Step-by-step production methods, process parameters, temperature cycles, and timing sequences.
  • 3
    Equipment configurations: Machine settings, custom tooling, equipment modifications, and production line layouts.

⚠ Why This Clause Matters

For the Disclosing Party: Your manufacturing trade secrets may represent decades of refinement and millions of dollars in R&D. A competitor learning your exact formula or process could replicate your product advantage in months rather than years. This clause ensures comprehensive protection for technical information that may not fit neatly into standard NDA definitions.

For the Receiving Party: Manufacturing trade secrets often overlap with general industry knowledge. You need clear boundaries so your engineers don't inadvertently violate the NDA by applying commonly known techniques. A poorly defined clause could create liability for using standard manufacturing practices.

Legal Considerations: Trade secret protection under the Defend Trade Secrets Act and state laws requires reasonable measures to maintain secrecy. A well-drafted NDA clause demonstrates you took appropriate steps to protect your information, which is essential if litigation becomes necessary.

📄 Clause Versions

Balanced Version: Provides comprehensive protection for manufacturing trade secrets while acknowledging the receiving party's right to use general industry knowledge. Suitable for partnerships, joint ventures, and vendor relationships where both parties contribute technical expertise. 
"Manufacturing Trade Secrets" means any non-public information relating to the Disclosing Party's manufacturing operations that derives independent economic value from not being generally known, including but not limited to:

(a) Formulas, compositions, and specifications for raw materials, intermediate products, and finished goods, including chemical compositions, ingredient ratios, and material grades;

(b) Manufacturing processes, including process flows, production sequences, operating parameters, temperature profiles, pressure settings, and timing requirements;

(c) Equipment configurations, including machine settings, tooling specifications, custom modifications, and production line layouts;

(d) Quality control procedures specific to the Disclosing Party's manufacturing processes, including testing protocols and acceptance criteria;

(e) Yield optimization techniques, waste reduction methods, and efficiency improvements developed by the Disclosing Party; and

(f) Any improvements, modifications, or derivatives of the foregoing developed during the term of this Agreement.

The Receiving Party acknowledges that Manufacturing Trade Secrets may be disclosed orally, visually (including facility tours), or in writing, and agrees to maintain their confidentiality regardless of the form of disclosure. Information disclosed orally or visually shall be confirmed in writing within thirty (30) days.

This clause does not restrict the Receiving Party's use of general manufacturing knowledge, skills, and experience that are commonly known in the industry and not specific to the Disclosing Party's operations.
Disclosing Party Favor: Maximum protection for proprietary manufacturing information. No carve-outs for general knowledge, no marking requirements, and explicit protection for all disclosed technical information. Best used when sharing highly sensitive processes with potential competitors or in acquisition due diligence. 
"Manufacturing Trade Secrets" means all technical and manufacturing information disclosed by or on behalf of the Disclosing Party, or observed by the Receiving Party during facility visits, demonstrations, or technical discussions, including without limitation:

(a) All formulas, compositions, blends, mixtures, and specifications, whether relating to raw materials, work-in-progress, or finished products, including all chemical compositions, compound structures, ingredient lists, material grades, and mixing ratios;

(b) All manufacturing processes, methods, techniques, and procedures, including process flows, operating parameters, production sequences, machine settings, temperature cycles, pressure levels, timing sequences, and any other process variables;

(c) All equipment information, including machine specifications, tooling designs, fixture configurations, jig designs, custom equipment modifications, automation sequences, and production line layouts;

(d) All quality control and quality assurance information, including testing methods, inspection criteria, defect classifications, corrective procedures, and statistical process control data;

(e) All yield, efficiency, and performance data, including production rates, waste metrics, downtime analyses, and optimization techniques;

(f) All supplier and material source information relating to manufacturing inputs;

(g) All cost information relating to manufacturing operations, including material costs, labor costs, overhead allocations, and margin data; and

(h) Any and all improvements, modifications, combinations, derivatives, or developments based on or derived from any of the foregoing.

All information disclosed in connection with this Agreement shall be presumed to be Manufacturing Trade Secrets unless the Disclosing Party explicitly states otherwise in writing. No marking, stamping, or labeling shall be required for information to receive protection under this Agreement.

The Receiving Party agrees that any knowledge, information, or techniques acquired through exposure to the Disclosing Party's manufacturing operations shall be deemed Manufacturing Trade Secrets, regardless of whether such information might be characterized as general knowledge or industry practice.
Receiving Party Favor: Narrow definition requiring specific identification and documentation of trade secrets. Protects the receiving party's ability to use general manufacturing knowledge and clearly distinguishes between proprietary and standard industry information. 
"Manufacturing Trade Secrets" means only that manufacturing-related information which:

(a) Is disclosed in writing or other tangible form and is clearly marked "Trade Secret," "Confidential Manufacturing Information," or with a similar designation at the time of disclosure;

(b) Is specifically identified in Exhibit A to this Agreement as a trade secret, with sufficient particularity to enable the Receiving Party to identify and segregate such information; and

(c) Meets the legal definition of a trade secret under applicable law, including deriving independent economic value from not being generally known and being subject to reasonable measures to maintain its secrecy.

For information disclosed orally or visually (including during facility tours), such information shall only be deemed a Manufacturing Trade Secret if:
(i) It is identified as a trade secret at the time of disclosure;
(ii) It is documented in a written summary delivered to the Receiving Party within ten (10) business days of disclosure; and
(iii) Such summary specifically describes the information with sufficient detail to enable identification.

The following shall NOT be considered Manufacturing Trade Secrets:
(a) General manufacturing principles, techniques, or practices that are commonly known in the relevant industry;
(b) Information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate was independently developed by its personnel without reference to the Disclosing Party's information;
(c) Standard equipment specifications, operating procedures, or settings that are publicly available from equipment manufacturers;
(d) Information that becomes generally known in the industry through no fault of the Receiving Party; and
(e) Information that the Disclosing Party fails to identify and document in accordance with this clause.

The Receiving Party's obligation to protect Manufacturing Trade Secrets shall not restrict its right to use general skills, knowledge, and experience in manufacturing, including industry-standard processes, commonly available equipment, and general engineering principles.

💬 Key Considerations

  • ✓
    Document everything disclosed. Maintain detailed records of what information was shared, when, and to whom. This documentation is essential for enforcement if a breach occurs.
  • ✓
    Control facility access. If disclosing trade secrets includes facility tours, implement visitor protocols, limit photography, and consider having visitors sign separate acknowledgments.
  • ✓
    Distinguish from patents. Trade secrets and patents offer different protections. Ensure your clause covers information you intend to keep as trade secrets, not information you plan to patent.
  • ✓
    Address employee mobility. Consider how this clause interacts with employee movement between companies and what happens when key personnel transition.
  • ✓
    Include improvement ownership. Clarify who owns improvements or modifications developed during the relationship, especially in joint development scenarios.
