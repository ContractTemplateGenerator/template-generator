Real-World Scenarios

How dataset licensing NDAs apply in common situations

🏢

Enterprise AI Company Licensing Medical Data

An AI company licenses medical imaging datasets for diagnostic model training. The NDA protects pricing from competitors and prevents the data provider from disclosing deal size to investors.

Tip: Include mutual confidentiality - provider wants pricing secret, licensee wants usage scope private.
📈

Exclusive Dataset for Competitive Advantage

A startup licenses exclusive access to a unique dataset. The NDA prevents the provider from disclosing the exclusivity arrangement to competitors who might bid for similar rights.

Tip: Ensure exclusivity extends to confidentiality about the exclusivity itself.
💰

Volume Discount Negotiations

A licensee negotiates significant volume discounts. The NDA prevents the provider from telling other customers about the discount tiers achieved.

Tip: Address aggregate disclosures carefully - providers may want to reference "major customers" without specifics.
📊

Data Marketplace Participation

A company evaluates datasets on a data marketplace. The NDA protects evaluation results and prevents disclosure of which datasets were considered or rejected.

Tip: Include provisions covering evaluation data and benchmark results, not just final licensing terms.

Negotiation Tips for Dataset Licensees

Key points when licensing AI training data

  • 1

    Limit Publicity Rights

    Restrict the provider's ability to name you as a customer in marketing materials, press releases, or investor presentations without approval.

  • 2

    Define Aggregate Disclosures

    Specify exactly what the provider can say in aggregate (e.g., "We have Fortune 500 customers") without revealing your specific deal terms.

  • 3

    Protect Evaluation Results

    Ensure confidentiality covers not just final terms but also your evaluation process, benchmarks, and reasons for selecting or rejecting datasets.

  • 4

    Include Securities Exception

    If you're a public company, negotiate the right to disclose material terms in securities filings when legally required.

  • 5

    Consider Survival Periods

    Pricing and competitive terms should remain confidential even after the license ends. Negotiate survival periods that match the competitive relevance of the information.
