Real-World Scenarios

How AI partnership NDAs apply in common collaboration situations

🏥

University-Industry Research Partnership

A tech company partners with a university to develop new ML algorithms. The NDA must balance academic publication rights with commercial protection and address student inventor rights.

Tip: Include pre-publication review periods and define patent cost sharing and revenue splits.
🌏

Multi-Company AI Consortium

Multiple companies form a consortium to develop shared AI infrastructure. The NDA must enable collaboration while protecting each company's competitive advantages.

Tip: Create clear "contribution zones" and define what improvements each party can use independently.
🚀

Startup-Enterprise Joint Development

A startup partners with an enterprise to integrate AI into existing products. The NDA protects the startup's core IP while enabling meaningful collaboration.

Tip: Avoid broad assignment clauses. Keep startup's background IP separate from joint developments.
💰

AI Investment Due Diligence

A VC gains access to AI company's models and data during diligence. The NDA must prevent sharing with portfolio competitors and restrict use even if investment doesn't proceed.

Tip: Include specific restrictions on sharing with other portfolio companies and define information barriers.

Negotiation Tips for AI Partnerships

Key points when entering AI collaborations

  • 1

    Document Background IP Thoroughly

    Before sharing anything, create a detailed inventory of your background IP with timestamps. This prevents disputes about what you brought versus what was jointly developed.

  • 2

    Specify Contribution Metrics

    Define how contributions will be measured: compute hours, lines of code, data volume, personnel time. This provides objective basis for IP allocation if disputes arise.

  • 3

    Address Personnel Movement

    What happens when employees switch between partner organizations? Define how knowledge can be used and restrict competitive hiring during and after the partnership.

  • 4

    Plan for Partnership Failure

    Define clear exit procedures: what each party keeps, how joint IP is handled, and whether continued development rights exist. Negotiate this when everyone is optimistic.

  • 5

    Include Governance Mechanisms

    Establish joint steering committees, dispute resolution procedures, and decision-making processes for key issues like IP allocation and commercialization.
