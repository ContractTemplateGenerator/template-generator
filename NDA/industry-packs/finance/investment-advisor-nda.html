⚖️ Key Provisions for Advisor NDAs

  • SEC Examination Carve-out
    Explicit permission for disclosure to SEC examiners, state securities regulators, and FINRA (for dual-registered advisors). Cannot restrict regulatory cooperation.
  • Form ADV Carve-out
    Allow disclosure of information required on Form ADV including disciplinary history, material conflicts, and business practices required to be publicly disclosed.
  • Fiduciary Disclosure Carve-out
    Permit disclosures required by fiduciary duty to clients, including conflicts of interest, compensation arrangements, and material facts affecting advice.
  • Client List Definition
    Specifically define client lists, prospect lists, and relationship information as confidential. Address what constitutes protectable trade secret status.
  • Strategy Replication Restriction
    Prohibit replication of proprietary investment strategies and model portfolios. Address derivative work restrictions for enhanced versions.
  • Residuals Limitation
    Strictly limit any residuals clause to exclude investment strategies, client information, and performance data from "general knowledge" carve-outs.

⚠️ Non-Solicitation Provisions: State Law Matters

Many advisor NDAs include non-solicitation provisions restricting the recipient from soliciting clients for a period after the relationship ends. However, enforceability varies significantly by state:

  • California: Generally unenforceable under Business & Professions Code Section 16600
  • New York: Enforceable if reasonable in scope, duration, and geography
  • Texas: Must be ancillary to an otherwise enforceable agreement
  • Florida: Presumptively valid if reasonable; two-year maximum common

Consult with an employment attorney in your jurisdiction before relying on non-solicitation provisions. Consider whether these belong in the NDA or a separate employment/consulting agreement.
