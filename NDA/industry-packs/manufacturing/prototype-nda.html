Prototype Sharing NDA

Protect physical prototypes, samples, and products under development with provisions for handling, photography, tooling ownership, and return requirements.

🔒 Key Provisions for Prototype NDAs

  • Physical Handling Requirements
    Define storage conditions, access limitations, and security measures for physical prototypes. Specify who may handle prototypes and under what circumstances they may be removed from designated locations.
  • Photography & Recording Restrictions
    Prohibit photography, video recording, 3D scanning, and detailed sketching of prototypes without express written consent. Address storage and deletion of any authorized images.
  • Tooling Ownership
    Clearly define ownership of molds, dies, fixtures, and tooling created during development. Address physical possession, modification rights, and whether tooling may be used for other customers.
  • Return/Destruction Procedures
    Establish specific procedures and timelines for returning or destroying prototypes and related materials upon project completion, termination, or request. Require written certification of destruction.
  • Derivative Work Restrictions
    Prohibit the receiving party from creating derivative works, filing patents, or claiming IP rights based on disclosed prototype information. Clarify that all improvements remain discloser's property.
  • Sample Quantity Limits
    Specify the exact number of prototypes or samples provided and prohibit creation of additional copies without authorization. Track all samples with serial numbers or identifiers.

When to Use This NDA

Common prototype sharing scenarios requiring specialized protection

🛠️ Contract Manufacturing Evaluation

When sharing prototypes with potential contract manufacturers for quoting, capability assessment, or pilot production runs.

🔍 Design Partner Collaboration

Working with design firms, engineering consultants, or development partners who need access to physical prototypes.

🤝 Customer Evaluation Programs

Beta testing, customer trials, or evaluation programs where pre-release products are shared for feedback.

🏭 Trade Show Displays

When prototypes will be displayed at trade shows, exhibitions, or private demonstrations to select audiences.

⚠️ Common Prototype NDA Risks

Issue Risk Level Recommended Action
No photography restrictions High Add explicit prohibition on all forms of visual recording including smartphones
Unclear tooling ownership High Specify that all tooling is owned by discloser and must be returned on demand
No sample tracking requirements Medium Require numbered samples and periodic inventory confirmation
Vague destruction procedures Medium Define specific destruction methods and require written certification
No derivative work restrictions High Prohibit patents, improvements, and derivative works without authorization

☑️ Prototype Handling Checklist

Ensure your prototype NDA addresses these key areas:

  • Specific description and serial numbers of all prototypes being shared
  • Physical storage requirements (locked cabinet, climate control, etc.)
  • List of authorized personnel who may access prototypes
  • Prohibition on photography, video, 3D scanning, and detailed drawings
  • Restrictions on removing prototypes from specified locations
  • Tooling, molds, and fixture ownership clearly assigned
  • Prohibition on creating additional copies or samples
  • Return procedures with specific timeline (e.g., 10 business days)
  • Destruction certification requirements if return not feasible
  • Prohibition on derivative works and patent filings
  • Provision for on-site inspection of prototype security
  • Insurance requirements for prototype loss or damage

⚠️ Consult an IP Attorney

Prototype sharing involves significant IP risks that go beyond NDA protection. Consider whether patent applications should be filed before disclosure, and whether trade secret protection is adequate for your situation. International prototype sharing raises additional concerns around foreign IP enforcement. Consult with an intellectual property attorney before sharing valuable prototypes.
