🔒 Key Provisions for Factory Tour NDAs

  • Visual Observation Coverage
    Explicitly define that confidential information includes anything observed during the visit: equipment, layouts, processes, worker activities, products in production, and facility organization.
  • Photography Prohibition
    Comprehensive ban on photography, video, 3D scanning, and any form of visual recording. Include provisions allowing search of personal devices if violation suspected.
  • Equipment & Layout Protection
    Protect the identity, configuration, and arrangement of manufacturing equipment. Factory layouts themselves can be valuable trade secrets.
  • Process Observation
    Address manufacturing processes, cycle times, quality procedures, and operational methods observed during the tour. These may not be documented but are still confidential.
  • Post-Visit Restrictions
    Prohibit visitors from using observations to develop competing products, advise competitors, or take any action that exploits observed confidential information.
  • Visitor Log Requirements
    Maintain detailed visitor logs documenting who visited, when, areas accessed, and escort assigned. This documentation supports trade secret protection claims.

📋 Recommended Visitor Protocol

☑️ Pre-Visit Checklist

  1. Collect signed NDA at least 24 hours before scheduled visit
  2. Verify visitor identity and company affiliation
  3. Screen for competitor relationships or conflicts
  4. Brief escort on areas to avoid and topics not to discuss
  5. Prepare visitor badge and access credentials
  6. Notify production areas of scheduled tour

☑️ During Visit Protocol

  1. Collect phones and cameras at security checkpoint
  2. Issue visitor badge with clear "ESCORT REQUIRED" marking
  3. Provide verbal reminder of NDA obligations
  4. Maintain constant escort presence throughout visit
  5. Avoid areas with sensitive IP not relevant to visit purpose
  6. Log all areas visited and approximate times

☑️ Post-Visit Procedures

  1. Return personal devices after visual inspection
  2. Collect visitor badge and deactivate credentials
  3. Complete visitor log with escort notes
  4. File NDA with visitor log for reference
  5. Follow up with visitor contact if needed

When to Use This NDA

Common factory visit scenarios requiring specialized protection

🔍 Customer Audits

When customers visit to audit quality systems, production capabilities, or compliance with their supplier requirements.

🤝 Potential Partners

When prospective business partners, investors, or acquirers visit to evaluate manufacturing capabilities.

🎓 Educational Tours

When hosting students, academic groups, or industry associations for educational facility tours.

👥 Competitor Visits

When industry peers visit for benchmarking, standards development, or association activities (use enhanced restrictions).

⚠️ Physical Security Matters

An NDA is only part of factory security. Consider physical security measures including visitor badges, escort requirements, camera-controlled areas, and security checkpoints. Trade secret protection requires demonstrating reasonable security measures - the NDA documents your confidentiality expectations, but physical controls demonstrate you take protection seriously.
