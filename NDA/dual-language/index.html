❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Why do I need a dual-language NDA? +
A dual-language NDA ensures both parties fully understand the confidentiality terms in their native language. This is essential for international business because it reduces disputes, ensures informed consent, and makes the agreement easier to enforce in foreign jurisdictions where the other party may have assets.
Which language version controls if there's a conflict? +
Our dual-language NDAs include a "controlling language" clause that you can customize. Options include: English controls (typical for US-based companies), the foreign language controls (when operating in that jurisdiction), or both versions are equally authoritative (for true bilateral relationships). We recommend choosing the language of the governing law.
Are bilingual NDAs legally enforceable? +
Yes, dual-language NDAs are legally enforceable when properly drafted. The key requirements are: (1) including a controlling language clause, (2) ensuring accurate legal translation of all terms, (3) having both parties sign the same document, and (4) following the formalities required by the governing law.
Should both language versions be in the same document? +
We recommend keeping both versions in a single document using either a side-by-side column layout or a sequential layout. This ensures both parties sign the same agreement and makes it clear that both versions are part of the same contract. Our export tool lets you choose the layout that works best for your situation.
What if my counterparty requests changes to one language only? +
Any changes must be reflected in both language versions to maintain consistency. If your counterparty requests changes to their language version, work together to agree on the meaning in both languages. Never sign an agreement where the two versions say different things - this creates ambiguity that could be exploited in a dispute.
