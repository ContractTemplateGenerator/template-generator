Executive Summary Active Dispute
▼

Apex Ventures LLC engaged ByteStream Solutions Inc. for custom enterprise software development under a $485,000 fixed-price contract executed March 15, 2025. The project involved building a proprietary inventory management system with AI-powered forecasting capabilities.

After multiple missed milestones and delivery of a product that failed acceptance testing, Apex withheld the final payment of $127,000. ByteStream has threatened litigation claiming the deliverables met specifications, while Apex maintains the software fails critical performance benchmarks outlined in the SOW.

Resolution deadline: January 15, 2026 (contractual dispute resolution period expires)

Detailed Issue Analysis

1 Material Breach Analysis Critical
▼

KEY QUESTIONS:

  • Does the failure to meet performance specifications constitute material breach?
  • Has ByteStream been given adequate opportunity to cure under Section 8.2?
  • Can Apex recover consequential damages for business disruption?

Breach Evidence Summary:

Specification Required Actual Status
API Response Time < 200ms 580ms average FAIL
Concurrent Users 500+ ~150 before timeout FAIL
Uptime SLA 99.9% 94.2% (test period) FAIL
Data Export All formats CSV only PARTIAL

Attorney Analysis

The documented performance failures likely constitute material breach under California law. The specifications were expressly bargained-for terms, and ByteStream's failure to meet them goes to the essence of the agreement. The cure period under Section 8.2 (30 days from written notice) should be triggered immediately to preserve all remedies.
