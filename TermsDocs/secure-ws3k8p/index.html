Dear Sir or Madam:

This firm represents Wendy Shaw regarding the 25-year Power Purchase Agreement ("PPA") she signed on November 9, 2020. This letter constitutes formal demand for RESCISSION of the PPA and removal of the solar system from Ms. Shaw's property based on multiple violations of California law and material misrepresentations during the sales process.

I. GROUNDS FOR RESCISSION

Ms. Shaw is entitled to rescind this contract under California Civil Code §§ 1689-1693 based on the following violations:

A. AB 1070 Disclosure Violations (Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 7169)

California's Solar Consumer Protection Act (AB 1070) mandates specific disclosures on the FIRST PAGE of any solar contract in a clear, conspicuous manner. Your PPA fails to comply:

  1. The cover page disclosure is materially incomplete - it omits the total contract cost, system size, and warranty information required by law.
  2. The actual total cost ($64,619.00 over 25 years) is buried on page 36 of 41 in print so small it requires a magnifying glass to read. This deliberate concealment defeats the purpose of AB 1070's consumer protection requirements.
  3. The required "California Solar Consumer Protection Guide" referenced in Section 2 of the PPA Disclosures was never provided to Ms. Shaw at signing or thereafter.

B. Undisclosed UCC-1 Fixture Filing

The PPA authorizes Sunnova to file a UCC-1 financing statement against Ms. Shaw's property (Section 11(d)). This was never disclosed or explained during the sales process. Ms. Shaw only learned of this filing - which clouds her title and complicates any sale or refinance - five years after signing. The salesman, Manuel Garcia, specifically assured Ms. Shaw that selling her home with the system would be "easy" - a representation that was false and known to be false given the UCC-1 filing.

C. Material Misrepresentations

Ms. Shaw was promised "incredible savings" on her electric bills. The reality:

  • Her electricity costs INCREASED 35.8% (25.8% after a 10% auto-pay discount) from 2020 to 2024 - the same rate increase experienced by NON-solar customers fully exposed to utility rate hikes.
  • Her total annual electricity cost rose from $1,926.76 (2020, pre-solar) to $2,409.97 (2024, with solar).
  • The system has UNDERPERFORMED the Schedule A production estimates every single year:
    - Year 1: 9.05% below estimate
    - Year 2: 6.18% below estimate
    - Year 3: 1.01% below estimate
    - Year 4: 5.32% below estimate

These are not the "estimates" of an honest company - they are inflated projections designed to induce consumers to sign.

D. Breach of Contract and Constructive Abandonment

The PPA (Section 4) prohibits Ms. Shaw from having anyone other than Sunnova/SunStrong service the system. Yet SunStrong has made itself completely unreachable:

  • Ms. Shaw spent 5 weeks attempting to contact SunStrong by phone, including one documented call of several calls where she was on hold for 2 hours and 48 minutes before giving up.
  • Four certified letters to four different SunStrong locations were ignored.
  • Multiple website inquiries received only automated responses directing her to call the same unanswered phone line.

A company that contractually prohibits customers from obtaining service elsewhere, while simultaneously making itself unavailable, has constructively abandoned its contractual obligations. This is not merely a breach - it renders the entire contract unenforceable.

II. DEMAND

Ms. Shaw demands the following within THIRTY (30) DAYS of the date of this letter:

  1. Full rescission of the PPA (Contract ID OB003396198);
  2. Removal of the solar system from Ms. Shaw's property at your expense;
  3. Termination and release of the UCC-1 financing statement;
  4. Refund of all payments made under the PPA;
  5. Release of any and all claims against Ms. Shaw arising from this contract.

III. CONSEQUENCES OF NON-COMPLIANCE

If you fail to comply with these demands, Ms. Shaw will:

  1. File a complaint with the California Attorney General's Office;
  2. File a complaint with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau regarding the undisclosed UCC-1 filing;
  3. File a complaint with the California Contractors State License Board;
  4. Initiate arbitration under PPA Section 19 seeking rescission, restitution, and all damages permitted under California's Unfair Competition Law (Bus. & Prof. Code § 17200), the Consumer Legal Remedies Act (Civ. Code § 1750 et seq.), and California Civil Code § 1689 et seq.;
  5. Seek recovery of attorney's fees as the prevailing party.

This letter also serves as the required notice under California Civil Code § 1782 for claims under the Consumer Legal Remedies Act.

LITIGATION HOLD

You are hereby directed to preserve all documents, communications, and electronically stored information relating to Ms. Shaw's account, the sales process, system performance data, and any complaints from other California customers regarding similar issues.

Govern yourselves accordingly.
