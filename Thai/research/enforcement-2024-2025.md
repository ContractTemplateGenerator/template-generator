# Thailand Foreign Nominee Structure Enforcement Actions (2024-2025)

**Research Date:** December 30, 2025
**Coverage Period:** January 2024 - December 2025

---

## Executive Summary

Thailand has launched its most aggressive enforcement campaign to date against illegal foreign nominee structures. As of 2025, authorities have undertaken over **29,000 legal cases** and prosecuted **852 companies** for nominee-related offenses, with estimated economic damages exceeding **THB 15.1 billion** (~$450 million USD). This document details specific enforcement actions, raids, criminal convictions, and emerging patterns.

---

## 1. Major Raids and Crackdowns by Location

### 1.1 Phuket Province

#### April 2024 - Initial Raids
- **Date:** April 4-5, 2024
- **Location:** Phuket Province (8 locations)
- **Agencies Involved:** Crime Suppression Division (CSD)
- **Target:** 17 companies suspected of Russian ownership using Thai nominees
- **Trigger:** Investigation of phone scam that led to discovery of shell company network
- **Key Finding:** Russian woman and two Thai women directors were shareholders in 38+ companies
- **Outcome:** Evidence collection, warrants obtained for further investigation

#### May 2024 - "Operation Nominee Sweep"
- **Date:** May 31, 2024
- **Location:** Phuket Province
- **Agencies Involved:** Central Investigation Bureau (CIB), Economic Crime Suppression Division (ECSD)
- **Scale:** Largest single operation to date
- **Arrests:**
  - 231 total arrests
  - 98 foreign suspects (mostly Russians)
  - 37 Thai nationals acting as nominee agents
  - 96 legal entities charged
- **Seized Assets (Total Value: THB 1.5 billion / ~$40.75 million USD):**
  - 225 bank accounts with THB 318.9 million turnover
  - 245 land documents (~10,500 sq meters, est. value THB 1 billion)
  - 43 land deeds (24 rai, est. value THB 200 million)
  - 196 passports
  - 108 work permits
  - 800 company registration documents
  - 1,601 company stamps

#### September-October 2024 - DSI Prosecution
- **Date:** September 11, 2024 (Criminal Court filing)
- **Outcome Announced:** October 10, 2024
- **Target:** Law and accounting firm orchestrating nominee arrangements
- **Scale:** Approximately 60 companies implicated
- **Convictions:** 23 defendants (Thai nationals, foreigners, Thai juristic persons, foreign juristic persons)
- **Sentences:** See Criminal Convictions section below

#### February 2025 - Continued Enforcement
- **Date:** February 2025

### DSI Collaboration Framework
The DSI operates in collaboration with:
- Department of Business Development (DBD)
- Tourism Department
- Ministry of Tourism and Sports
- Immigration Bureau
- Anti-Money Laundering Office (AMLO)
- Economic Crime Suppression Division (ECSD)
- Department of Lands (DOL)

### Investigation Methodology
The DSI investigates four key streams:
1. **Funding Trail:** Whether Thai shareholders have verifiable, independent sources for share subscriptions
2. **Control Signals:** Who authorizes payments, signs contracts, sets policy (email trails, board minutes, mandates)
3. **Benefit Flow:** Whether profits are distributed to Thais proportionate to equity, or leak back to foreign side via fees
4. **Filing Consistency:** Cross-referencing DBD records, tax returns, payroll data, immigration/work-permit files, UBO/AML disclosures

## 3. Criminal Convictions and Sentences

### Landmark Case: Phuket Law and Accounting Firm (September 2024)

**Case Details:**
- **Filing Date:** September 11, 2024
- **Court:** Criminal Court of Thailand
- **Defendants:** 23 (mix of Thai nationals, foreigners, Thai juristic persons, foreign juristic persons)
- **Companies Involved:** ~60 companies

**Sentences Imposed:**
- **Initial Sentence:** 10 years imprisonment
- **Reduced Sentence:** 5 years imprisonment (reduced due to cooperation, confessions, no prior criminal record)
- **Suspension:** Sentence suspended for 2 years
- **Probation:** 1 year
- **Fine:** THB 200,000 per defendant
- **Corporate Penalty:** Companies ordered to be dissolved
- **Additional Orders:** Thai defendants ordered to cease nominee activities

### Statutory Penalties Available

#### Foreign Business Act (FBA)
- **Section 36 (Anti-Avoidance):**
  - Up to 3 years imprisonment
  - Fines up to THB 1 million
  - Daily penalties up to THB 50,000 for continued violations

- **Section 37 (Illegal Business Operation):**
  - Up to 3 years imprisonment
  - Fines THB 100,000 to THB 1,000,000
  - Court-ordered business stoppage or dissolution
  - Daily fines THB 10,000-50,000 for violating court orders

- **Section 41 (Director/Partner Liability):**
  - Directors, partners, or authorized representatives face up to 3 years imprisonment
  - Fines THB 100,000 to THB 1,000,000

#### Land Code
- **Section 111:**
  - Up to 2 years imprisonment
  - Fines up to THB 20,000
  - Applies to foreigners acquiring land illegally (personally or through nominees)

- **Sections 112-113:**
  - Same penalties for Thai nationals/juristic persons acting as agents for foreigners

- **Sections 94 & 96:**
  - Forced disposal of illegally held land within 180 days to 1 year

#### Criminal Code
- **Section 267 (False Statements):**
  - Up to 3 years imprisonment
  - Fines up to THB 6,000
  - For providing false statements during land registration

## 4. FBA Sections 36-37 Enforcement Patterns

### Section 36 - Anti-Avoidance Provisions

**Prohibited Conduct:**
- Thai nationals aiding foreigners to engage in restricted business without permission
- Acting as nominee shareholders to circumvent FBA requirements
- Holding shares to enable foreign business operation

**Enforcement Trends 2024-2025:**
- Shift from paper compliance checks to substance-over-form analysis
- Focus on actual control and funding sources
- Courts consistently upholding illegality of structures masking foreign control
- Real-time data integration between DBD and Revenue Department

### Section 37 - Business Operation Violations

**Application:**
- Foreigners operating businesses in violation of Sections 6, 7, and 8 (List 1, 2, 3 restrictions)
- Court-ordered cessation of business operations
- Mandatory company dissolution

### Enforcement Statistics (September 2024 - January 2025)
- **Cases Investigated:** 820
- **Economic Damages Identified:** THB 12.5 billion
- **Extended Period (Sept 2024 - May 2025):** 861 cases, THB 15.3 billion damages

## 5. Land Code Violations Enforcement

### Active Enforcement Measures 2024-2025

**Land Department Actions:**
- Regular audits of companies with foreign connections
- Compulsory sale orders for land held in violation of nominee restrictions
- "Source of Funds" interviews with Thai shareholders
- Cross-referencing with DBD and tax records

**Consequences of Detection:**
- Transaction voided
- Forced land disposal ordered
- Criminal prosecution of directors/shareholders
- No compensation for confiscated land under proposed amendments

### Notable Land Code Case: Catherine Delacote (2024)

**Background:**
- French businesswoman who passed away April 29, 2024, in Koh Samui
- 7-month investigation following her death
- Assets included luxury villa and adjacent land worth THB 50 million

**Findings:**
- Companies tied to Delacote used Thai nominees
- Structure circumvented laws prohibiting foreign land ownership
- Highlighted that enforcement continues posthumously

### Proposed Nominee Transactions Act
- Would criminalize indirect control
- Allow confiscation of land without compensation
- Create 15-year prescription period for predicate offenses

## 7. Red Flags That Trigger Investigations

### Financial Red Flags

1. **Inadequate Capital Sources**
   - Thai shareholders lacking proof of funds for share subscriptions
   - No verifiable income source for claimed investments

2. **Dividend Distribution Anomalies**
   - Absence of proportionate dividend income to Thai shareholders
   - Profits not matching equity positions

3. **Suspicious Payment Patterns**
   - Disguised management fees paid to foreigners
   - Consulting/royalty fees that leak profits to foreign parties
   - Bank records showing nominee shareholders receiving payments from foreigners

### Structural Red Flags

4. **Passive Thai Shareholders**
   - Same Thai individuals appearing in multiple unrelated companies
   - Thai shareholders with no genuine business involvement (taxi drivers, housekeepers as majority shareholders)
   - Shareholders unable to explain business operations

5. **Corporate Filing Inconsistencies**
   - Discrepancies between DBD records and tax returns
   - Mismatches in payroll data and work-permit files
   - Conflicting UBO/AML disclosures

6. **Control Signal Indicators**
   - Foreigners signing contracts, authorizing payments, setting policy
   - Email trails showing foreign decision-making
   - Board minutes indicating foreign control

### Timing/Behavior Red Flags

7. **Sudden Ownership Changes**
   - Ownership transfers before applying for Foreign Business License (FBL)
   - Restructuring in anticipation of enforcement actions

8. **Registration Patterns**
   - Rapid increase in company registrations (e.g., Phuket Russian companies)
   - Shell companies with no genuine business activity

### AI-Powered Detection

**Automated Flags:**
- Algorithms identify high-risk patterns
- Multiple companies using same passive Thai shareholders
- "Dormant" company patterns
- Cross-referencing of multi-agency databases in real-time

## 9. Proposed Legal Reforms (2024-2025)

### Anti-Money Laundering Act Amendments

**Status:** Passed legislative process and public hearing (confirmed August 16, 2024); submitted to Cabinet for House approval

**Key Changes:**
- **Nominee as Predicate Offense:** Acting as or assisting nominee structure = money laundering
- **AMLO Asset Seizure Powers:** Expanded to cover assets from both direct offenders and those who support/facilitate
- **15-Year Prescription Period:** Allows prosecution of older violations

### Foreign Business Act Amendments

**Proposed Changes:**
- Redefine "foreigner" to include those controlling companies through Thai representatives
- Clear definitions for "nominee" and "nominee transaction"
- Close loopholes allowing indirect foreign ownership

### Nominee Transactions Act (Proposed)
- Criminalize indirect control
- Allow land confiscation without compensation
