Criminal Law Guide for Foreigners in Thailand

Understanding defamation laws, police procedures, vaping penalties, and other criminal matters that catch foreigners off guard in Thailand.

Important Notice for Foreigners

Thailand's criminal justice system differs significantly from Western systems. Key differences that surprise many foreigners include:

  • Criminal defamation: Truthful statements can still be criminally defamatory under Thai law
  • Strict liability offenses: Some violations carry criminal penalties regardless of intent
  • Bail restrictions: Foreigners may face stricter bail conditions or passport surrender
  • Online content: Social media posts made anywhere can trigger Thai criminal jurisdiction

Key Criminal Statutes

Important criminal laws that frequently affect foreigners in Thailand

Penal Code Sections 326-333

Criminal defamation provisions. Even true statements can be defamatory if they damage reputation.

Up to 2 Years Prison

Computer Crime Act (2007, amended 2017)

Criminalizes false data entry, content deemed harmful to national security, and certain online speech.

Up to 5 Years Prison

Customs Act - ENDS Devices

Import ban on e-cigarettes and vaping devices. Possession and use also restricted under separate orders.

Fines + Confiscation

Penal Code Section 112

Lese-majeste law protecting the monarchy. Extremely serious charges with strict penalties.

Up to 15 Years Prison

Immigration Act Sections 12, 54

Overstay penalties, deportation provisions, and re-entry bans for immigration violations.

Fines + Deportation + Bans

Narcotics Act (Category 5)

Cannabis regulations under the evolving 2024-2025 framework. Possession limits and licensing requirements.

Varies by Violation
