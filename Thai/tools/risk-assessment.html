Business Structure Risk

Assess risks related to company ownership, Thai shareholders, and Foreign Business Act compliance

The Foreign Business Act restricts foreign ownership in certain sectors.

Authorities investigate whether Thai shareholders actually invested their own money.

Control mechanisms like sole signing authority or side agreements are red flags.

Passive shareholders who only appear on paper are a major red flag.

Property Risk

Evaluate risks related to land ownership, nominee arrangements, and lease structures

Foreigners generally cannot own land in Thailand. Company ownership is scrutinized.

Standard registered leases max at 30 years. Longer arrangements have varying enforcement.

Creative structures like usufruct combinations or preference shares are under scrutiny.

Visa & Immigration Risk

Assess risks related to visa status, overstay, work permits, and immigration compliance

Your visa type determines what activities you can legally perform in Thailand.

Past overstays affect future visa applications and can result in entry bans.

Work is broadly defined. Even "helping" a business can be illegal without permit.

Repeated short trips to reset visa exemptions are viewed as abuse.

Criminal Exposure Risk

Assess risks from activities that could result in criminal charges under Thai law

Defamation and lese-majeste laws are strict. Truth is NOT a defense for defamation.

Business disputes often escalate to criminal complaints in Thailand.

E-cigarettes and vaping are illegal in Thailand with serious penalties.

Cannabis rules changed in 2024. Public use is restricted, extracts regulated.

Tax Risk

Evaluate risks related to tax residency, foreign income, and reporting compliance

180+ days makes you a Thai tax resident - significant since 2024 rules.

Since 2024, all foreign income remitted is taxable (no same-year rule).

Tax residents with assessable income must file returns by March 31.

Required for tax residents and those with Thai income.
