Professional & licensed businesses

Attorneys, CPAs, health-care providers, engineers, and other licensees can organize as an LLC, but the licensing board may require PLLC filings, professional liability endorsements, or prior approval. Compare PLLC requirements with professional corporations in the corporation guide before choosing format.

Foreign owners & interstate operations

Non-U.S. members should plan for withholding on effectively connected income, FIRPTA for real estate dispositions, and potential branch profits tax. Cross-border structures often require blocker corporations or treaty planning‚Äîcoordinate before signing real estate contracts.

LLCs operating outside Virginia must foreign-qualify in those states. Similarly, out-of-state LLCs doing business in Virginia need a certificate of registration (LLC-1052), registered agent, and annual $50 fee. Failing to qualify voids contracts and bars court actions until cured.

Operating agreement & series governance

Clauses I always explain to clients:

  • Management & voting: Define which decisions require unanimous consent (admitting new members, dissolving, series creation) versus majority, and specify manager/officer authority.
  • Economic terms: Targeted capital accounts, preferred returns, distribution waterfalls, and series allocations to keep tax lawyers happy and auditors calm.
  • Transfer & buy-sell: Drag/tag rights, right of first refusal, mandatory redemption on death/disability, and deadlock resolution to avoid chancery litigation.
  • Fiduciary modifications: Virginia permits reasonable limitation of duties‚Äîmake clear when managers can pursue outside opportunities and how conflicts are disclosed.
  • Noncompetition & restrictive covenants: Narrow geographic/scope to survive Virginia‚Äôs reasonableness test; tie to severance and buy-out mechanics.
  • Series governance: Document how each protected series is created, who runs it, and what happens if accounts are commingled or assets move between series.

Series LLCs & business trust alternatives

Virginia‚Äôs protected-series statute lets an LLC isolate assets, but the shield only works if each series behaves like a separate business. Maintain separate ledgers, bank accounts, insurance endorsements, leases, and contracts for each protected series. Clearly label property records and digital assets with the series name.

Protected series example

Three rental properties can live inside one umbrella LLC with three protected series: Series A (Arlington condo), Series B (Richmond duplex), Series C (Norfolk storefront). Filing cost: $100 for the LLC + $50 per series; annual: $50 √ó 4.

Compare that to three separate LLCs at $100 each to form and $50 each annually. Some lenders, however, still demand standalone LLC borrowers, so budget for the conservative path if financing is imminent.

Business trust alternative

Funds and multiseries asset managers often prefer a Virginia business trust (Title 13.1, Chapter 12.1). Trustees owe fiduciary duties similar to directors, but lenders recognize the structure more readily.

Taxes & PTE election

Virginia‚Äôs pass-through entity (PTE) tax allows partnerships, S corps, and multi-member LLCs to pay tax at the entity level and provide owners with a credit. Coordinate with a CPA‚ÄîPTE elections interact with nonresident withholding, composite returns, and credit-for-tax-paid rules in other states. It is not available to C-corp‚Äìelected LLCs.

Single-member LLCs remain disregarded unless they elect S or C status; remember that payroll taxes and Virginia unemployment thresholds apply even if the entity is disregarded federally.

CTA, packages & pitfalls

As of March 26, 2025, FinCEN‚Äôs interim final rule treats domestic Virginia LLCs as non-reporting companies. That could change‚Äîpolitical and judicial challenges are ongoing. Foreign-formed LLCs registering in Virginia still file BOI reports within 30 days of SCC registration unless another exemption applies. Reconfirm BOI requirements before closing transactions or restructuring ownership.

Common pitfalls (law-office perspective)

Piercing the veil: Commingled funds, undocumented insider loans, or ignoring the operating agreement invite chancery courts to disregard the LLC. Keep capital reasonably proportional to liabilities and record related-party deals in writing.

Tax misalignment: Your operating agreement must match the election you file. Targeted capital accounts and distribution waterfalls for a partnership do not automatically work once you elect S-corp status.

Foreign qualification blind spots: Signing contracts or hiring employees in other states without registering exposes you to penalties and bars you from state courts. Budget for multi-state registration if you operate interstate.

Registered agent complacency: Discount RA services sometimes forward service of process slowly. If you name yourself but travel often, you risk missing lawsuits or SCC notices. Budget for a reliable agent with E-service capability.
