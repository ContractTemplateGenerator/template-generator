Blockchain-Based Trading Platform License Requirements

Updated Dec 2024 25 min read Licensing & Registration

The Licensing Labyrinth

Launching a blockchain-based trading platform in the United States requires navigating one of the most complex regulatory environments in financial services. Unlike traditional brokerages that follow a relatively clear licensing path, crypto and blockchain platforms face overlapping federal and state requirements, uncertain jurisdictional boundaries, and regulators actively competing for authority.

In my practice advising trading platform founders, I have seen well-funded projects abandon US market entry entirely due to licensing complexity, while others have successfully launched by implementing strategic licensing approaches tailored to their specific business models.

This guide provides a comprehensive roadmap to the licensing requirements facing blockchain trading platforms, from federal registration to state-by-state analysis, with practical guidance on costs, timelines, and strategic considerations.

Enforcement Without Clarity

Regulators have pursued enforcement actions against unlicensed platforms even as the licensing requirements remain unclear. The SEC, CFTC, FinCEN, and state regulators have all taken action against crypto trading platforms. Operating without proper licenses exposes founders to personal liability, platform seizure, and criminal prosecution.

ATS Registration for Blockchain Platforms

Alternative Trading Systems (ATS) provide a pathway for blockchain platforms to operate within the SEC's regulatory framework without full national securities exchange registration. For platforms trading digital asset securities, ATS registration may be the most viable federal licensing option.

What Is an ATS?

An ATS is any organization, association, or system that constitutes, maintains, or provides a marketplace for bringing together purchasers and sellers of securities but does not set rules governing subscriber conduct (other than conduct on the platform) or discipline subscribers other than by exclusion from trading.

ATS Registration Requirements

To operate as an ATS, a platform must:

  • Register as a broker-dealer: File Form BD with FINRA and become a member firm
  • File Form ATS: Submit initial operation report at least 20 days before commencing operations
  • Comply with Regulation ATS: Meet requirements for fair access, capacity, integrity, and confidentiality
  • Implement surveillance: Establish systems to monitor trading for manipulation and fraud
  • Maintain records: Keep detailed records of all transactions and communications

Form ATS-N for NMS Stock ATSs

Platforms trading NMS stocks must file Form ATS-N, which requires extensive public disclosure about operations, trading procedures, and potential conflicts of interest. While most crypto platforms do not trade NMS stocks, this enhanced disclosure regime may eventually extend to digital asset securities.

Crypto-Specific ATS Challenges

Blockchain platforms face unique challenges in the ATS framework:

  • Custody requirements: Traditional broker-dealer custody rules do not map cleanly to digital assets
  • Settlement timing: Blockchain settlement differs from traditional T+2 settlement
  • Token classification: Unclear which tokens qualify as securities requiring ATS treatment
  • Market surveillance: On-chain activity creates new surveillance challenges

National Securities Exchange Requirements

Full registration as a national securities exchange under Section 6 of the Exchange Act represents the most comprehensive federal licensing pathway. While no crypto-native platform has achieved this status, understanding these requirements provides context for the regulatory ceiling.

National Exchange Requirements

  • Self-regulatory organization: Must establish rules and enforce member compliance
  • Fair access: Cannot unreasonably deny access to qualified participants
  • Capacity and security: Robust technology infrastructure
  • Market surveillance: Real-time monitoring for manipulation
  • Listing standards: Requirements for listed securities
  • Registration timeline: 18-36 months typical

Why This Matters for Crypto

  • Legitimacy: Exchange status confers maximum regulatory credibility
  • Institutional access: Many institutions can only trade on registered exchanges
  • Legal certainty: Clear regulatory framework reduces litigation risk
  • Competition: Coinbase, Gemini pursuing exchange licenses
  • Future-proofing: Prepares for likely regulatory requirements
  • Cost: $10M+ total investment typical

The SEC's 2022 Proposed Rule

In January 2022, the SEC proposed amendments to Rule 3b-16 that would expand the definition of "exchange" to explicitly include systems using "communication protocols" to bring together buyers and sellers. This proposal targets DeFi protocols and automated market makers.

If adopted, the rule would require most blockchain trading platforms to either:

  • Register as a national securities exchange
  • Register as an ATS (requiring broker-dealer registration)
  • Qualify for an exemption

Rulemaking Status

The proposed rule remains pending as of December 2024. The comment period generated significant industry opposition, and final adoption remains uncertain. However, platforms should prepare for a regulatory environment where exchange registration may be required.

Multi-State Licensing Strategy

Most platforms pursue a phased approach to state licensing:

Recommended Licensing Phases

  • 1
    Phase 1: Core Markets (6-12 months)
    Target the largest states: California, Texas, Florida, Illinois. These represent ~40% of US population and establish credibility for subsequent applications.
  • 2
    Phase 2: New York BitLicense (12-24 months)
    Apply for BitLicense after establishing operational track record. New York approval signals regulatory legitimacy to institutional customers.
  • 3
    Phase 3: National Coverage (18-36 months)
    Expand to remaining states systematically. Consider Nationwide Multistate Licensing System (NMLS) for efficient multi-state applications.
  • 4
    Phase 4: Optimization
    Evaluate Wyoming SPDI charter for custody operations. Consider federal charter options as they become available.

BitLicense (NY) Deep Dive

New York's BitLicense remains the most demanding state crypto license, serving as a de facto national standard. Obtaining BitLicense approval signals regulatory credibility and opens access to the New York market and New York-based institutional investors.

BitLicense Requirements

  • Application fee: $5,000 (non-refundable)
  • Compliance program: Written policies for AML, cybersecurity, business continuity
  • Capital requirements: Determined by DFS based on business model
  • Surety bond: Amount determined by DFS
  • Background checks: Fingerprinting for all principals, executive officers, and significant shareholders
  • Books and records: Comprehensive recordkeeping requirements
  • Custody: Must hold customer virtual currency in trust
  • Cybersecurity: 23 NYCRR 500 compliance required

BitLicense Application Timeline

Pre-Application (2-4 months) Develop compliance program, gather documentation, prepare application materials
Application Submission Submit comprehensive application via NMLS with all required exhibits
Initial Review (3-6 months) DFS reviews application completeness, requests additional information
Background Investigation (3-6 months) Fingerprinting, background checks, financial condition review
Substantive Review (6-12 months) Detailed evaluation of business plan, compliance program, technology
Approval/Denial (Total: 18-36 months) DFS issues decision; conditional approval may require additional steps

BitLicense Alternatives

Companies may alternatively apply for a New York limited purpose trust company charter, which provides broader banking powers. Paxos, Gemini, and others have obtained trust charters rather than BitLicenses. Trust charters require $5 million minimum capital but provide greater operational flexibility.

FINRA Broker-Dealer Requirements for Crypto

Platforms trading digital asset securities must register as broker-dealers with the SEC and become FINRA member firms. This requirement applies to platforms facilitating the purchase, sale, or exchange of tokens that qualify as securities under the Howey test.

Broker-Dealer Registration Process

  1. Form BD filing: Submit Uniform Application for Broker-Dealer Registration through FINRA Gateway
  2. FINRA membership application: File Form NMA and supporting materials
  3. State registrations: Register in each state where conducting business
  4. Principal and representative exams: Series 7, 24, 63 and other qualifications
  5. Written supervisory procedures: Comprehensive compliance manual
  6. Net capital compliance: Meet minimum net capital requirements

Net Capital Requirements

Market maker $1,000,000

Special Purpose Broker-Dealer Framework

In December 2020, the SEC issued a statement providing a 5-year no-action pathway for "special purpose broker-dealers" focused exclusively on digital asset securities. This framework allows broker-dealers to custody digital assets under modified custody rules, but requires adherence to specific operational limitations and reporting requirements.

FINRA Crypto-Specific Guidance

FINRA has issued several regulatory notices addressing crypto activities:

  • Reg Notice 19-24: Guidance on customer margin requirements for digital assets
  • Reg Notice 21-25: Supervision requirements for crypto-related communications
  • Reg Notice 22-08: Guidance on retail communications about crypto

Custody Solutions & Qualified Custodian Requirements

For platforms serving institutional clients or investment advisers, custody of digital assets presents unique regulatory challenges. The "qualified custodian" requirement under the Investment Advisers Act has significant implications for crypto trading platforms.

Qualified Custodian Standards

Under SEC Rule 206(4)-2 (the "custody rule"), investment advisers must maintain client assets with a "qualified custodian," which includes:

  • Banks and savings associations
  • Registered broker-dealers
  • Registered futures commission merchants
  • Foreign financial institutions meeting specific requirements

State Trust Company Path

  • Wyoming SPDI: Purpose-built for digital assets
  • NY Trust Charter: DFS-regulated option
  • SD Trust Charter: Emerging crypto-friendly option
  • Timeline: 12-24 months
  • Capital: $5M+ typically
  • Benefit: Qualified custodian status

Federal Charter Options

  • OCC National Bank: Full banking powers
  • OCC Trust Bank: Trust and custody focus
  • Federal Reserve: Master account access
  • Timeline: 18-36+ months
  • Capital: $20M+ typically
  • Benefit: Nationwide operation

2023 SEC Custody Rule Proposal

In February 2023, the SEC proposed amendments to the custody rule that would expand its scope to all client assets (not just "funds and securities") and impose enhanced requirements on crypto custodians. Key provisions include:

  • Reasonable assurance standard: Custodians must provide reasonable assurance of asset protection
  • Written agreement requirement: Detailed custody agreements with specific protections
  • Surprise examination enhancements: Modified requirements for crypto-specific risks
  • Segregation requirements: Clear asset segregation mandates

Wyoming SPDI Charter Option

Wyoming's Special Purpose Depository Institution (SPDI) charter represents the most crypto-friendly bank charter in the United States. For trading platforms requiring custody solutions, the SPDI provides a viable pathway to qualified custodian status.

SPDI Key Features

  • Digital asset custody: Explicitly authorized to custody digital assets
  • No FDIC insurance required: Operates on 100% reserve basis
  • Payment services: Can provide payment and remittance services
  • Fiduciary services: Trust and fiduciary powers available
  • Federal Reserve access: Eligible for Federal Reserve master account (application required)

Custodia Bank (formerly Avanti)

SPDI Charter Granted 2020 | Fed Master Account Denied 2023

Background: Custodia Bank, founded by Caitlin Long, received an SPDI charter in September 2020 and applied for Federal Reserve master account access in October 2020.

Outcome: In January 2023, the Federal Reserve denied Custodia's master account application, citing concerns about the novel business model and risks to the payment system. Custodia has filed suit challenging the denial.

Implications: The Custodia case demonstrates that obtaining an SPDI charter does not guarantee Federal Reserve access. Platforms should plan operations without assuming Fed access will be granted.

FinCEN MSB Registration for Trading Platforms

Nearly all blockchain trading platforms operating in the United States must register as Money Services Businesses (MSBs) with the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN). This federal registration is separate from and in addition to state licensing requirements.

MSB Registration Requirements

FinCEN defines an MSB to include money transmitters, which encompasses businesses that accept and transmit currency or value that substitutes for currency. Crypto exchanges and trading platforms generally qualify as money transmitters.

  • Registration deadline: Must register within 180 days of starting business
  • Registration fee: Free (no filing fee)
  • Registration renewal: Every two years
  • Registration form: FinCEN Form 107

BSA/AML Compliance Program

MSBs must implement a comprehensive Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance program including:

  1. Written policies and procedures: Tailored to business model and risk profile
  2. Designated compliance officer: Individual responsible for day-to-day compliance
  3. Employee training: Initial and ongoing AML training
  4. Independent review: Periodic testing by independent party
  5. Customer identification program (CIP): Verify identity of customers
  6. Customer due diligence (CDD): Understand customer relationships
  7. Suspicious activity monitoring: Systems to detect and report suspicious activity
  8. SAR filing: Suspicious Activity Reports for transactions over $2,000
  9. CTR filing: Currency Transaction Reports for transactions over $10,000
  10. Recordkeeping: 5-year retention of transaction and customer records

Failure to Register

Operating as an unregistered MSB is a federal crime under 18 U.S.C. 1960, carrying penalties of up to 5 years imprisonment. FinCEN has pursued numerous enforcement actions against crypto businesses operating without proper registration, with civil penalties often exceeding $100 million.

Travel Rule Compliance

Under the FinCEN Travel Rule (31 CFR 1010.410), MSBs must collect, retain, and transmit certain information for transfers of $3,000 or more:

  • Name of transmitter and recipient
  • Account numbers (if any)
  • Address of transmitter
  • Amount of transmission
  • Identity of recipient's financial institution

Recent Enforcement Precedents

Understanding recent enforcement actions provides critical insight into regulatory priorities and the consequences of non-compliance.

In re Binance (2023)

FinCEN, DOJ, OFAC | November 2023

Facts: Binance operated as the world's largest crypto exchange while allegedly failing to register with FinCEN, maintain adequate AML programs, or comply with US sanctions.

Outcome: $4.3 billion total settlement. CEO Changpeng Zhao pled guilty to BSA violations and resigned. Binance agreed to comprehensive compliance undertakings.

Key Lessons:

  • US jurisdiction extends to foreign platforms serving US customers
  • AML/sanctions compliance failures carry existential consequences
  • Personal criminal liability attaches to executives
  • Retroactive geographic blocking does not cure past violations

SEC v. Coinbase (2023)

SEC Civil Action | June 2023

Allegations: SEC alleges Coinbase operates as an unregistered securities exchange, broker, and clearing agency by facilitating trading in crypto asset securities.

Status: Litigation ongoing. Coinbase has moved to dismiss, arguing tokens listed are not securities.

Implications: This case will likely establish key precedents regarding which tokens constitute securities and whether crypto exchanges must register with the SEC.

NYDFS v. Robinhood Crypto (2022)

NYDFS Consent Order | August 2022

Facts: NYDFS found significant BSA/AML compliance failures at Robinhood Crypto's New York operations, including inadequate transaction monitoring and OFAC screening.

Outcome: $30 million penalty plus requirement to retain independent consultant and implement remediation program.

Key Lessons:

  • State regulators actively enforce crypto compliance
  • BitLicense holders face heightened scrutiny
  • Compliance program deficiencies trigger enforcement even absent specific violations

Kraken Staking Settlement (2023)

SEC Settlement | February 2023

Facts: SEC alleged Kraken's staking-as-a-service program constituted an unregistered securities offering.

Outcome: $30 million penalty. Kraken agreed to discontinue US staking services.

Key Lessons:

  • Staking services may be securities requiring registration
  • SEC willing to pursue yield-generating crypto products
  • Service discontinuation may be required as part of settlement

Practical Implementation Steps

Based on my experience guiding platforms through the licensing process, I recommend the following implementation approach:

Pre-Launch Phase (6-12 months before launch)

  1. Business model documentation: Clearly define services, customer types, and geographic scope
  2. Token analysis: Conduct Howey analysis on all tokens to be listed
  3. Licensing strategy: Determine federal vs. state approach based on business model
  4. Corporate structure: Establish appropriate legal entities and governance
  5. Compliance infrastructure: Begin building AML/KYC systems and policies
  6. FinCEN registration: Register as MSB immediately

Initial Launch Phase

  1. Priority state applications: File in largest accessible states
  2. Geographic restrictions: Block users in states where not licensed
  3. Compliance monitoring: Implement ongoing transaction monitoring
  4. Regulatory engagement: Establish relationships with key regulators

Expansion Phase

  1. New York application: File BitLicense when operationally ready
  2. Additional states: Systematically expand state coverage
  3. Federal options: Evaluate broker-dealer or ATS registration if trading securities
  4. Custody solution: Consider trust charter for institutional custody

Key Success Factors

The platforms that successfully navigate US licensing share common characteristics: realistic timelines (plan for 24+ months to nationwide operation), adequate capitalization ($5M+ for serious market participation), experienced compliance leadership, and proactive regulatory engagement. Cutting corners on compliance to achieve faster launch consistently results in enforcement actions and ultimately greater delays and costs.
