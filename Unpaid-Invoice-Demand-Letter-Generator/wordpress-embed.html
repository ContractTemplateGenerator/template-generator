💻 SaaS Subscription Non-Payment 🚨

Client continues using service but stops paying monthly/annual fees

▶️
🎯 SaaS Strategy: Leverage service suspension as immediate pressure while pursuing payment for past usage.
⚡ Immediate Actions:
  • Service suspension: Suspend access immediately upon payment failure
  • Data retention notice: Inform client of data deletion timeline
  • Usage documentation: Capture logs showing continued platform usage
  • Contract enforcement: Reference specific payment terms from SaaS agreement
💡 SaaS-Specific Leverage:
  • Business dependency: Client likely depends on your service for operations
  • Integration costs: Switching providers is expensive and time-consuming
  • Data hostage: Their data/configurations are valuable assets
  • Recurring relationship: They want to continue the service relationship
🛡️ Generator Coverage

Select "SaaS/Subscription Services" in work description. The generator emphasizes service suspension, data retention policies, and the business disruption caused by non-payment.

SaaS Demand Language:
"Your continued use of our SaaS platform while failing to pay constitutes breach of contract and unjust enrichment. We have suspended your account and will permanently delete your data in [X] days unless payment is received immediately."

👨‍💻 Freelancer Project Non-Payment 💪

Completed project work but client refuses to pay final invoice

▶️
🏆 Freelancer Power: Your work product gives you significant leverage in collection efforts.
🚫 Client Stall Tactics (Don't Accept):
  • "Need minor revisions": Scope creep to avoid payment
  • "Waiting for approval": Indefinite internal process delays
  • "Cash flow issues": Their problem, not yours
  • "Quality concerns": Raising issues after acceptance
⚡ Freelancer Enforcement Tools:
  • Copyright retention: You own the work until paid
  • Platform leverage: Report to Upwork, Fiverr, etc.
  • Public reviews: Honest feedback about payment issues
  • Industry reputation: Word spreads in professional networks
🛡️ Generator Coverage

The generator includes freelancer-specific language about copyright retention, platform reporting, and professional reputation damage for non-paying clients.

Freelancer Rights Language:
"As the creator of this work, I retain all copyright and intellectual property rights until payment is received in full. Continued use of my work without payment constitutes copyright infringement and will be reported to relevant platforms and professional networks."

🔨 Contractor/Construction Payment ⚖️

Completed construction/renovation work with unpaid final invoice

▶️
🔥 Lien Power: Contractors have the strongest collection tools - mechanic's liens can force property sales to collect payment.
⚡ Mechanic's Lien Process:
  • Preliminary notice: File within 20 days of starting work (varies by state)
  • Lien filing: Must file within 60-120 days of completion
  • Lien enforcement: Can force property sale to collect payment
  • Priority position: Liens often have priority over other debts
💰 Additional Contractor Rights:
  • Stop work orders: Halt project until payment current
  • Material supplier liens: Suppliers can also lien the property
  • Bond claims: Claim against payment/performance bonds
  • Prompt payment laws: Many states require quick payment
🛡️ Generator Coverage

Check "File Mechanic's Lien" option. The generator emphasizes lien rights and includes specific construction industry language about property encumbrance.

Lien Threat Language:
"As a contractor who has improved your property, I have the right to file a mechanic's lien for unpaid amounts. This lien will encumber your property title and may prevent sale or refinancing until satisfied. Lien filing deadline: [DATE]."

📞 Client Communication Breakdown 📵

Client stops responding to calls, emails, and payment requests

▶️
📬 Documentation Strategy: Create a paper trail showing your collection efforts and their avoidance.
📋 Communication Documentation:
  • Email read receipts: Prove they received your messages
  • Certified mail: Legal proof of delivery
  • Phone call logs: Date, time, outcome of calls
  • Social media activity: Shows they're active but ignoring you
🎯 Breaking the Silence:
  • Multiple channels: Email, phone, mail, LinkedIn, etc.
  • Third-party contacts: Colleagues, partners, mutual connections
  • Public pressure: Professional platform reviews/comments
  • Legal service: Process server for formal notices
🛡️ Generator Coverage

Include your previous communication attempts in the "Previous Attempts" section. The generator creates urgency by highlighting their avoidance behavior.

Communication Breakdown Language:
"Your failure to respond to our repeated attempts to contact you regarding this overdue debt demonstrates bad faith. This formal demand letter serves as final notice before escalating to collection agencies and legal action."

⏰ Net 30/60/90 Terms Violation 📋

Client violates agreed payment terms in contract or invoice

▶️
📋 Contract Power: Clear payment terms give you legal grounds for immediate collection action.
⚡ Payment Terms Enforcement:
  • Breach of contract: Violation of agreed payment schedule
  • Late fees: Enforce penalty clauses immediately
  • Interest charges: Compound daily from due date
  • Acceleration clauses: Make entire balance due immediately
💪 Contract Leverage Points:
  • Attorney fees clause: They pay your collection costs
  • Jurisdiction clause: Force litigation in your preferred court
  • Personal guarantees: Hold individuals personally liable
  • Security interests: Rights to collateral or assets
🛡️ Generator Coverage

The generator automatically calculates days overdue and references contract breach. Include your original payment terms and any penalty clauses in the work description.

Contract Breach Language:
"Your failure to pay within the agreed Net [X] terms constitutes material breach of our contract. This triggers late fees of [X]%, interest charges, and our right to accelerate the entire outstanding balance to immediate payment."
