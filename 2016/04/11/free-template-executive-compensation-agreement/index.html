Understanding Executive Compensation Agreements

An Executive Compensation Agreement outlines the terms and conditions of employment between a corporation and its executive officers. Key components include:

Key Elements:

  • Term of Employment: Duration of the agreement with renewal provisions
  • Base Compensation: Salary tied to company metrics (e.g., MRR, ARR) or fixed amount
  • Bonus Structure: Performance-based, discretionary, or guaranteed bonuses
  • Equity Compensation: Stock options subject to performance milestones and vesting schedules
  • Benefits: Vacation, sick days, health insurance, and other perks
  • Duties: Scope of responsibilities and working hours
  • Termination: Conditions for termination and severance provisions
  • Property Rights: IP assignment and confidentiality obligations

Important Considerations:

Executive agreements should be tailored to the specific circumstances of the company and executive. Consider market rates, company stage, and performance metrics when structuring compensation.
