âš–ï¸ Legal Background on Quitclaim Deeds

A quitclaim deed transfers whatever interest the grantor holds in the propertyâ€”without warranties of title, liens, or encumbrances. Unlike warranty deeds, quitclaims offer no guarantees that the grantor has clear title or that the title is free of defects.

This makes quitclaim deeds particularly common in:

  • Transfers between spouses, including post-marriage or divorce.
  • Gifts of property between family members.
  • Correction deeds to fix errors in legal descriptions or ownership.
  • Transfers into or out of living trusts or business entities.
  • Estate planning, such as pre-death transfers to children or co-owners.
