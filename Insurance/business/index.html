Business Insurance Disputes

Business insurance is fundamentally different from personal insurance. The policies are more complex, the stakes are higher, and insurers often take more aggressive positions when denying or limiting coverage. Whether you are facing a CGL dispute, a professional liability claim, or a business interruption issue, understanding your policy and rights is crucial.

Commercial policies contain intricate coverage grants, exclusions, and conditions that interact in complex ways. Insurance companies exploit this complexity to deny legitimate claims. The duty to defend in CGL policies, the "claims-made" versus "occurrence" distinction, and policy period issues all create potential disputes.

This guide covers the major types of business insurance and common disputes that arise. I help business owners understand their coverage, challenge wrongful denials, and recover what their policies promise.

CA

California Business Insurance Law

Special rules for California businesses

California has specific rules that affect business insurance claims. The duty to defend is particularly broad in California, and bad faith claims against commercial insurers can result in significant damages. California Insurance Code section 790.03 prohibits unfair claims practices, and businesses can pursue bad faith claims just like individual policyholders.
