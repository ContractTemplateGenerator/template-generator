How long does PayPal legally have to hold my funds?
The User Agreement doesn't specify a maximum hold period, which is problematic. However, Delaware's implied covenant of good faith limits PayPal's discretion. Holds extending months without specific justification violate this covenant. Standard holds are 21-180 days, but indefinite holds can be challenged through arbitration for breach of implied covenant.
Can I skip the 45-day notice and file arbitration immediately?
No. Section 14.3 of the User Agreement requires pre-arbitration informal dispute resolution. Failing to send proper notice to PayPal's Legal Department and wait 45 days will get your arbitration dismissed on procedural grounds. The notice requirement is mandatory and strictly enforced by AAA.
What if PayPal claims I violated their Acceptable Use Policy?
Demand specifics: which policy provision and what facts support the violation. PayPal's AUP is vague. If they can't cite specific violations with supporting evidence, the hold violates the implied covenant. Your demand letter should challenge vague justifications and demand concrete evidence.
Do I need a lawyer to file AAA arbitration?
No. AAA Consumer Rules are designed for self-represented parties. The AAA administrator guides you through procedures. Arbitrators accommodate unrepresented parties. Our generator creates filing-ready arbitration demands. For straightforward holds with good documentation, self-representation works well. Consider an attorney for amounts over $25K or complex legal issues.
What's my downside if I lose in arbitration?
You're responsible for your $200 filing fee plus any evidence costs (typically under $50). AAA Consumer Rules don't allow fee-shifting to losing parties unless claims were frivolous or in bad faith. PayPal won't recover their $8K-12K in fees from you. Your total risk is around $250-300, making arbitration low-risk for legitimate claims.
How do I prove damages beyond the held amount?
Documentation is key: Bank statements showing late fees with dates, credit card bills showing penalty charges, emails/contracts showing lost business opportunities, credit reports showing score drops, vendor communications showing damaged relationships. Quantify everything with specific dollar amounts and dates linking to the PayPal hold.
Will PayPal retaliate by closing my account?
PayPal cannot legally retaliate for exercising arbitration rights. However, they can terminate accounts for any non-discriminatory reason. Practically, your PayPal relationship may end after arbitration. They must still release your funds (minus legitimate offsets). Be prepared to use alternative payment processors if you rely on PayPal for business.
What if PayPal settles during the 45-day notice period?
This is success! If PayPal releases funds during the notice period, you achieved your primary goal without arbitration. You can still pursue consequential damages if substantial, or accept the principal release as sufficient resolution. Many cases settle within 30 days once PayPal sees a professional demand with arbitration attachment.
