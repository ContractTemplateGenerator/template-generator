Digital product “competition” stops when your file is copied, rebranded, and sold. This console helps you separate real copying from coincidence, fire DMCA takedowns, and send a focused demand letter that pushes resellers to remove, disgorge, or pay.

What Counts as Theft

Copy-paste of your templates, lightly tweaked colors, bundles using your files = copyright infringement + breach of “personal use only” licenses.

Idea vs Expression

Generic concepts aren’t protected. Your wording, layout, structure, and compiled assets are.

Leverage Sources

Proof of creation, publication dates, marketplace listings, license terms, sales data, and copyright registration (if available).

Remedy Paths

Platform takedowns, direct demand letters, Copyright Claims Board for small U.S. claims, or full litigation for larger stakes.

Mindset: Treat your template like software—track versions, embed identifiers, and be ready to show a judge (or infringer) exactly what you made and when.

Automatic Copyright

Original digital downloads are protected the moment you create them. Registration boosts remedies but isn’t required to demand removal.

License Limits

“Personal use only / no resale” terms create contract hooks. Violations can be both breach and copyright infringement.

DMCA Safe Harbor

Platforms avoid liability by honoring proper takedown notices. Use that to yank listings fast.

Small-Claims Option

Copyright Claims Board lets you pursue up to $30k without federal court overhead.

Registration Timing
Register key products within three months of publication or before infringement pops up. That unlocks statutory damages and fee recovery if you ever sue.
Proof of Copying
Compile side-by-side comparisons, metadata, and sales timestamps. Independent creation defenses crumble when structure + text match line-for-line.

Platform Lane (DMCA)

Send compliant notices to hosts/marketplaces. Include signature, work ID, infringing URLs, contact info, good-faith + perjury statements.

Infringer Lane (Demand)

Write directly to the seller when you want assurances, payment, or data about past sales. DMCA removes; letters negotiate.

When to Combine

Takedown first to stop ongoing harm, then demand letter for backpay/destruction promises. Copy platform reps when helpful.

Limitations

DMCA is U.S.-centric but covers many global platforms. For local sites with no DMCA process, direct letters and payment processors might be your first line.

Reminder: A proper DMCA notice doesn’t require registration. Use it even while you prep your letter.
Response Strategy: Demand + DMCA Copying is blatant, seller traceable, and sales ongoing → send platform takedown and demand letter seeking removal + accounting.

Demand Letter Skeleton

Subject: Unauthorized Sale of [Product]

Hi [Name],

I own [Product] (launched [date]) sold via [your shop]. Your listing “[title]” on [platform] copies my template (structure, text, files match). You don’t have a license to distribute or resell it; my terms limit buyers to personal use.

To resolve this, please by [deadline]:
1. Remove the listing and stop all sales.
2. Confirm destruction of remaining copies.
3. Provide sales count + gross revenue from the infringing product.
4. Propose either a retroactive license or payment to settle past use.

If I don’t hear from you, I’ll pursue DMCA takedowns, payment-processor complaints, and, if needed, formal copyright claims.

– [You]
Add-ons
Attach screenshots, list prior DMCA notices, or include a short DMCA-style paragraph if you’re copying the platform. Keep tone firm but professional.

Evidence Checklist

Your creation proof
Original files with timestamps, drafts, published listing dates.
License terms
Screenshot/PDF of “personal use only / no resale” language.
Infringing listing
URLs, screenshots, downloaded copies, order confirmations.
Comparison deck
Side-by-side visuals showing identical layouts/text.
Sales indicators
Bestseller badges, review counts, visible sales numbers or price points.
DMCA log
Copies of notices sent, responses, counter-notices if any.
