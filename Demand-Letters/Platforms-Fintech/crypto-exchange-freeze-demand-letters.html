Crypto Exchange Freeze Demand Letters

Compliance reviews, AML holds, and OFAC screenings can freeze your fiat and crypto balances for weeks. Use this playbook to document legitimate source-of-funds, push for timelines, and escalate when a review drags on.

Common Freeze Triggers
Trigger Exchange Action Resolution Window
Large inbound transfer from new wallet Hold pending source-of-funds interview 3-14 days if documents provided
Rapid in-and-out transfers Flagged for layering/mixing risk 7-30 days, sometimes permanent closure

Why demand letters matter

  • Escalate beyond front-line support to legal/compliance teams.
  • Document cooperation and show that funds are legitimate.
  • Create record for regulators if the freeze violates policies.

Realistic expectations

  • OFAC or law-enforcement holds cannot be overridden by a demand letter.
  • Compliance teams can release funds once risk questions are answered.
  • Letters can secure partial withdrawals or timelines when reviews drag on.
Do not ask the exchange to violate AML obligations. Focus on transparency, documentation, and timelines instead of demanding immediate release without review.
Regulatory & Contract Framework
AML / BSA
  • U.S. exchanges operate as money services businesses under the Bank Secrecy Act. They must monitor transactions, file Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs), and sometimes hold funds while reviews occur.
  • FinCEN guidance on convertible virtual currency lists red flags such as mixers, darknet markets, and rapid movement through new accounts.
Sanctions (OFAC)
  • If funds are linked to sanctioned persons or jurisdictions, exchanges must block the property and report to OFAC within ten business days. Releases require OFAC authorization.
  • Demand letters can still request clarity: Is this a blocking obligation or an internal risk hold?
Exchange Agreements
  • User agreements reserve broad rights to suspend or close accounts. Highlight conflicting sections that promise timely communication or withdrawal rights.
  • Most agreements require arbitration. Demand letters preserve the record and can lead to negotiated releases before arbitration is filed.
Consumer protection overlay: Regulators scrutinize fintechs that freeze funds for months without explanation. Pointing to state UDAP standards and CFPB/FTC expectations on communication often moves compliance teams.
Documentation Checklist

Account History

  • Account opening date, KYC level, jurisdiction.
  • Past volume, average balances, trading pairs used.
  • Prior compliance reviews or tickets.

Source of Funds

  • Bank statements showing fiat deposits.
  • On-chain explorers linking deposits to regulated exchanges.
  • Invoices, payroll statements, or sale agreements for larger inflows.
Communication Trail
  • Support ticket numbers, email threads, chatbot transcripts.
  • Requests for documents and proof of your responses.
  • Any statements that funds would be released by a certain date.
Partner Bank Considerations
  • If fiat balances sit with a partner bank, note the bank's identity. Some disputes fall under banking regulators or the CFPB.
  • Gather account and routing numbers, statements, and any FDIC references the exchange used in marketing.
Letter Strategy
Tone & Objectives
  • Emphasize cooperation: "I am providing the following documents and request an updated review timeline."
  • Ask whether the freeze stems from sanctions, SAR obligations, or internal policy.
  • Request partial releases if certain assets are clearly unrelated to the flagged activity.
  • Document hardship (payroll, tax payments) without accusing the exchange of wrongdoing.
Key Sections
  1. Background: Account age, verification level, transaction history.
  2. Freeze timeline: Dates of deposits, restriction notices, and support contacts.
  3. Compliance acknowledgment: Recognize AML/BSA duties while requesting clarity.
  4. Documentation summary: Highlight source-of-funds exhibits.
  5. Specific requests: Determination letter, release timeline, partial withdrawals, escalation contact.
  6. Escalation notice: Potential arbitration, regulator complaints, or legal claims for breach of contract/unfair practices if no response.
Law-enforcement holds: If the exchange states that law enforcement requested the freeze, ask for contact information and case number. They may only release funds when authorities permit it.
