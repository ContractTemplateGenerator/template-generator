What is a MedSpa Demand Letter?

A MedSpa demand letter is a formal written request for compensation after a botched cosmetic procedure. When treatments like Botox, fillers, laser treatments, or chemical peels go wrong, you have the right to seek damages for medical expenses, corrective procedures, pain and suffering, and lost wages. A well-crafted demand letter is often the first step toward resolution—and frequently results in settlement without litigation.

Common MedSpa Injuries and Complications

  • Dermal filler migration — Filler moving from injection site causing asymmetry or lumps
  • Botox complications — Drooping eyelids, frozen expressions, or allergic reactions
  • Laser burns — Improper settings or technique causing scarring or hyperpigmentation
  • Chemical peel damage — Too-strong solutions causing permanent skin damage
  • Infection — Non-sterile equipment or technique leading to serious infections
  • Nerve damage — Numbness, tingling, or loss of sensation from improper injection

Who Can Be Held Liable?

Liability may extend to the practitioner who performed the procedure, the supervising physician (if a non-physician performed the treatment), the MedSpa business entity, and potentially the product manufacturer if a defective product caused the injury. Many MedSpas operate with minimal physician oversight—this can strengthen your case if an unqualified practitioner performed your procedure.

Damages You Can Recover

  • Medical expenses — Emergency care, corrective procedures, medications
  • Future treatment costs — Ongoing care, revision surgeries, scar treatment
  • Lost wages — Time off work for treatment and recovery
  • Pain and suffering — Physical discomfort and emotional distress
  • Disfigurement — Permanent scarring or changes to appearance
  • Refund — Cost of the original botched procedure

Document Everything

Your demand letter is only as strong as your evidence. Photograph injuries immediately and throughout healing. Keep all receipts for medical care. Request copies of your treatment records, consent forms, and before/after photos from the MedSpa. Save all communications. If possible, get a second opinion from a board-certified dermatologist or plastic surgeon documenting the substandard care you received.
