Don't Panic - Common Myths Debunked

✗

MYTH: "A demand letter means I'm being sued"

TRUTH: A demand letter is just a letter. No court is involved yet. It's often the first step before any legal action.

✗

MYTH: "I have to pay whatever they demand"

TRUTH: You don't have to pay anything until you're ordered by a court. Demands are negotiable.

✗

MYTH: "Ignoring it will make it go away"

TRUTH: Ignoring usually makes things worse. A professional response can often resolve the matter quickly.

✗

MYTH: "They have an attorney, so they must be right"

TRUTH: Anyone can hire an attorney to send a letter. The claims still need to be valid.

✗

MYTH: "Their deadline is legally binding"

TRUTH: Most deadlines in demand letters are arbitrary. Real statutory deadlines are different.

✗

MYTH: "I should respond immediately"

TRUTH: Take time to gather facts and plan your response. A hasty response can hurt your position.

What NOT To Do

🚫 Don't Ignore It

Ignoring a demand letter can escalate the situation. The sender may proceed to file a lawsuit, and your silence could be used against you as evidence of guilt or admission.

🚫 Don't Respond Emotionally

No matter how outrageous the claims seem, keep your response professional and factual. Angry or threatening responses can hurt your case and create new legal issues.

🚫 Don't Admit Fault

Be careful with your words. Phrases like "I'm sorry" or "I should have..." can be construed as admissions. Stick to facts and avoid apologizing for the underlying dispute.

🚫 Don't Destroy Evidence

Once you receive a demand letter, you have an obligation to preserve relevant documents, emails, and other evidence. Destruction can lead to serious legal consequences.

🚫 Don't Post on Social Media

Avoid discussing the dispute online. Social media posts can be used as evidence and may harm your negotiating position or legal defense.

🚫 Don't Miss Actual Legal Deadlines

While many demand letter deadlines are arbitrary, some reference real statutory deadlines. If you're unsure, consult an attorney immediately.

When Should You Get Attorney Help?

While many demand letters can be handled on your own, some situations call for professional legal assistance. Consider scheduling a consultation if any of these apply:

✓
The amount demanded exceeds $10,000
✓
You're a business facing contract claims
✓
The letter threatens a lawsuit
✓
Employment or discrimination claims
✓
You have potential counterclaims
✓
The claims involve fraud or intentional acts
✓
You're unsure about legal deadlines
✓
Insurance coverage questions
