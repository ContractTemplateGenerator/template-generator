Why a Demand Letter Works Against Stripe

Stripe is headquartered in San Francisco. A letter from a California attorney citing California law gets attention.

California Law Applies to Stripe Disputes

Stripe, Inc. is a California corporation. When you send a demand letter citing California's Unfair Competition Law (Bus. & Prof. Code 17200), money transmission regulations, and their own Terms of Service, Stripe's legal team takes notice.

Unlike a support ticket that disappears into a queue, a formal demand letter creates a documented legal record. Many disputes that seemed hopeless through normal channels resolve within weeks of receiving attorney correspondence.

  • California law protects against arbitrary fund holds
  • Stripe must follow money transmission licensing requirements
  • Their TOS has specific dispute resolution procedures
  • Documented legal pressure escalates to decision-makers

Stripe's legal department is based in San Francisco. A demand letter from a California-licensed attorney signals serious intent and local jurisdiction expertise. Common Stripe Disputes I Handle

These are the most frequent issues merchants face with Stripe. Each has specific legal leverage points.

🚫

Account Termination Without Notice

Stripe closed your account citing "high risk" or "TOS violation" with no specifics. You deserve an explanation and opportunity to cure before losing your payment processing.

Most Common
🔒

Reserve Fund Holds (6+ Months)

Stripe is holding a percentage of your revenue in "reserve" with no clear release date. Some merchants have funds locked for over a year. This may violate money transmission laws.

High Value
💳

Chargeback Disputes

You submitted compelling evidence but Stripe ruled against you anyway. The chargeback process often favors cardholders, but procedural errors can be challenged.

💰

Processing Fee Disputes

Unexpected fees, rate increases without proper notice, or charges that don't match your agreement. Fee disputes can often be recovered through formal demand.

⏰

Payout Delays

Your payouts are stuck "pending" or "under review" for weeks or months. Stripe must provide reasonable timelines for fund releases.

📋

Documentation Demands

Stripe keeps requesting more documents with no end in sight. There are limits to what they can reasonably demand before releasing your funds.
