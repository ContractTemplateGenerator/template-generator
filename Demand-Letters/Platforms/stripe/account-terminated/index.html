The Problem: Stripe's Termination Practices

Too many legitimate businesses face sudden account closures with no real explanation or recourse.

🚫

Termination With Little or No Notice

Stripe can and does close accounts without warning. You might wake up to find your ability to process payments completely cut off, with no advance notice to prepare alternative solutions.

❔

Vague "Violation of Terms" Cited

When Stripe does provide a reason, it is often a generic "violation of our Terms of Service" with no specifics about what you allegedly did wrong. How can you fix something when you do not know what it is?

🔒

Funds Held for 90-180 Days

After termination, Stripe typically holds your funds in reserve for 90 to 180 days. For many businesses, this creates a devastating cash flow crisis that threatens their survival.

😞

Frustrating, Opaque Appeals Process

Trying to appeal through Stripe's support channels often feels like shouting into a void. Form responses, long wait times, and ultimate rejection without meaningful explanation are common.

Your Rights Under California Law

Stripe is headquartered in San Francisco, California. This gives you significant legal leverage.

California Law Protects You

Stripe, Inc. is a California corporation subject to California's robust consumer and business protection laws. The California Business and Professions Code Section 17200 prohibits unfair, unlawful, and fraudulent business practices.

Additionally, California's implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing applies to your agreement with Stripe. They cannot act arbitrarily in terminating your account or holding your funds.

  • Stripe is subject to CA Business & Professions Code 17200
  • Your contract includes an implied covenant of good faith
  • California money transmission laws regulate fund holds
  • You may have claims for conversion if funds are held improperly
⚖ Potential Legal Claims

What You May Be Entitled To

  • Breach of Contract: If Stripe failed to follow their own termination procedures or acted without proper cause
  • Unfair Business Practices: CA Bus. & Prof. Code 17200 violations for arbitrary or deceptive termination
  • Conversion: If Stripe is holding your funds beyond what is legally permitted or without justification
  • Breach of Good Faith: California contracts require parties to deal fairly with each other
