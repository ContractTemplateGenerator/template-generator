üìã Three-Pass Audit Process for Claude Letters
Pass 1: Eliminate Hedging & Concessions
  • Highlight hedging words: Mark every "appears," "may," "might," "could," "arguably," "potentially," "seems"
  • Find empathetic concessions: Flag phrases acknowledging other party's perspective or difficulties
  • Identify mutual fault language: Remove statements suggesting shared responsibility
  • Remove apologetic tone: Delete overly diplomatic language that weakens position
Example Rewrites:
Before:
"It appears that the delivered services may not have fully met the contractual specifications."
After:
"You failed to deliver the services specified in Section 3.2 of our agreement."
Pass 2: Front-Load Your Demand
Effective 4-Paragraph Structure:
¬∂1: State problem directly: "This letter demands [specific action] regarding your breach of [agreement] dated [date]."
¬∂2: Core facts (3-5 sentences max): Brief chronology focused only on establishing breach
¬∂3: Specific demand + deadline: "I demand that you [action] by [date]."
¬∂4: Consequences: "If I do not receive [demand] by [deadline], I will [specific next step]."
Pass 3: Replace Vague Legal Language
  • Find vague phrases: "applicable laws," "relevant regulations," "established frameworks," "appropriate statutes"
  • Replace with contract language: Quote specific contractual obligations that were breached
  • Verify any statutory references: If citing law, verify it exists, applies, and provides claimed remedy
  • Delete unsupported legal claims: Remove if you can't cite specific source
