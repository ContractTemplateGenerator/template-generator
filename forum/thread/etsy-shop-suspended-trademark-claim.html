LisaT_IPLawyer Attorney Jul 23, 2025

IP attorney here. Let's break down what's actually happening and your options.

Understanding trademark infringement claims:

  • A trademark protects brand names, logos, and slogans - not entire product categories or design styles
  • "Minimalist geometric earrings" is likely too generic/descriptive to be a valid trademark on its own
  • Even if their trademark "GEOMETRIC LUXE" is valid, you would only infringe if you used confusingly similar branding that could mislead consumers

First step - verify the claim:

  1. Search the USPTO database (uspto.gov/trademarks) for their trademark
  2. Check: Is it registered? For what goods/services? What's the actual mark?
  3. Compare their trademark registration to what you're actually doing

If you never used their brand name or anything confusingly similar, this is likely a bogus claim.

LisaT_IPLawyer Attorney Jul 23, 2025

Based on what you've described, this sounds like trademark bullying - using IP claims to suppress legitimate competition.

For the counter-notice, include:

  • Statement that you have a good faith belief the claim is erroneous
  • Explanation that you use your own brand "Aria Handmade Jewelry," not their "GEOMETRIC LUXE" mark
  • Evidence: screenshots of your listings showing your branding, not theirs
  • Citation that style/design descriptors (like "minimalist" or "geometric") are not protectable
  • Photos of your original designs with creation dates if possible

The risks of filing a counter-notice:

  • If you file, Etsy will likely forward it to the claimant
  • They then have 10 business days to file a lawsuit against you, or Etsy will restore your shop
  • If they sue, you'll need to defend yourself (costly, but you can countersue for malicious prosecution)
  • If you file a false counter-notice knowing you're infringing, you could be liable for damages

In your case, the risk seems low. They would have to prove in court that your use of generic descriptive terms creates consumer confusion with their brand. That's a very weak case.

TrademarkExpert_Mike Jul 24, 2025

Trademark paralegal here. What they're doing is called "trademark trolling" and it's rampant on Etsy, Amazon, and other platforms.

The problem is that platforms like Etsy are terrified of liability under the DMCA and Lanham Act, so they take down first and ask questions later. The burden then falls on you to fight back.

Key point: trademark rights are LIMITED to the specific mark (the brand name/logo), not the entire product category. You have every right to sell geometric earrings and describe them as such.

Their trademark gives them the exclusive right to use "GEOMETRIC LUXE" as a brand name for jewelry. It does NOT give them the right to prevent anyone else from selling or describing geometric jewelry.

LisaT_IPLawyer Attorney Jul 24, 2025

On the held funds: Etsy's policy is typically to hold funds for 45-90 days after a suspension, but if your shop is reinstated via counter-notice, they should release them sooner. Check your Etsy Payment Account for the specific hold period. You may need to contact Etsy support to expedite release after reinstatement.

On contacting the claimant: I generally advise AGAINST direct contact. Here's why:

  • Anything you say can be used against you if they sue
  • They might try to intimidate you into staying down
  • It won't speed up the process - the formal counter-notice is what matters

Let the process work. File your counter-notice through Etsy, and let them forward it to the claimant. Then it's on the claimant to either sue or back down.

Pro tip: After this is resolved, consider filing a complaint with Etsy against the claimant for abuse of the IP reporting system. Etsy claims to penalize false reports, though enforcement is inconsistent.
