Angel wants to put $75K into my AI startup. She said she's fine with either a SAFE or a convertible note, my choice. I've read the YC materials but still confused on when one is better than the other.

Context: pre-revenue, plan to raise a seed round in 12-18 months, Delaware C-corp.

Depends on the investor honestly. Some angels prefer notes because they're "real debt" - psychologically feels more secure. But practically, SAFE is easier for everyone.

JM
JenM_Atty Attorney Nov 5, 2024

Key differences:

SAFE: Not debt, no interest, no maturity date. Converts to equity at your next priced round (or other trigger events). Founder-friendly because there's no deadline pressure.

Convertible Note: Is debt with interest (usually 5-8%) and a maturity date (usually 18-24 months). If you don't raise before maturity, technically the investor could demand repayment (though most negotiate an extension).

In practice, almost no angel actually demands repayment at maturity - they'd rather convert to equity or extend. But it IS technically their right with a note. I've seen it create awkward situations where founders feel pressured. SAFE avoids this entirely.

Leaning SAFE. One more question - post-money or pre-money SAFE?

YC
YC_Alum_2019 Nov 6, 2024

Post-money is the new standard from YC (since 2018). It's cleaner for calculating dilution. With pre-money SAFEs, every new SAFE dilutes all the previous ones and it gets confusing fast. Post-money makes the math predictable.

DK
DilutionKing Nov 6, 2024

one caveat on post-money: it's actually slightly MORE dilutive to founders because the cap includes the investment itself. so a $4M post-money cap is roughly equivalent to ~$3.5M pre-money depending on how much you raise. Just be aware.

JM
JenM_Atty Attorney Nov 7, 2024

Agree with post-money being cleaner despite slightly more dilution. The clarity is worth it. Also make sure you use the official YC SAFE - I've seen "custom" SAFEs with weird provisions snuck in. Stick to the standard.
