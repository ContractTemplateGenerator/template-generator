FTC Actions Against Similar Schemes

+

While Jacob Levinrad has not been sued by the FTC, the FTC has taken action against very similar e-commerce coaching schemes in Florida. These cases establish that the business practices alleged in complaints against Jacob Levinrad are the same practices the FTC considers fraudulent.

FTC v. Steven J. Mayer (2024-2025)

Scheme: Lunar Capital Ventures, Ecom Genie, Profitable Automation

Location: Southern District of Florida

Allegations:

  • Used social media ads touting "success" of clients claiming tens of thousands monthly
  • Claimed consumers could generate "$100K+ per month"
  • Consumers charged tens of thousands, sometimes cashing retirement accounts
  • Consumers "made no money at all and lost all of their initial investment"

Result: Permanent bans, assets turned over for consumer redress (March 2025)

FTC Press Release

FTC v. Ganadores / Robert Shemin (2025)

Scheme: Ganadores ecommerce and real estate coaching

Location: Middle District of Florida

Allegations:

  • Pitched "infallible system" to replace day jobs
  • Consumers paid "tens of thousands of dollars" for training
  • Coaching "did not live up to those promises"

Result: Lifetime ban from industry, assets forfeited
