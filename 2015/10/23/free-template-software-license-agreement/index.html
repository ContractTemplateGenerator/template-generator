Understanding Software License Agreements

A Software License Agreement is essential for protecting your software and defining the relationship between you and your users. Key aspects include:

1. It's a License, Not a Sale
The subscriber doesn't get any ownership rights to the program. This is a fundamental distinction that protects your intellectual property.

2. Intellectual Property Rights
Subscriber retains all ownership and IP rights to their data. Company retains all rights to the software program.

3. Usage Restrictions
Subscribers may not modify or reverse engineer the program. This prevents competitors from copying your code.

4. Liability Limitations
Software is provided without warranties, "as is." Company's maximum liability is typically capped at fees paid in the last 12 months.
