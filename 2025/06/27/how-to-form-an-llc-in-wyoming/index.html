üõ°Ô∏è Charging Order Protection Explained
‚ñº
  • What it is: Wyoming's strongest asset protection feature‚Äîif a creditor sues you personally and wins, they can ONLY get a "charging order"
  • Charging order limits creditor to: Receiving distributions IF the LLC makes any (you as manager control this)
  • Creditor CANNOT: Force distributions, seize LLC assets, vote, manage, or access bank accounts
  • Single-member LLCs protected: Wyoming extended charging order protection to single-member LLCs in 2010 (most states don't)
  • Phantom income trap: Creditor may owe tax on undistributed profits‚Äîoften forces favorable settlement
  • Real estate use case: Each property in separate Wyoming LLC = creditor suing over Property A cannot reach Properties B, C, D
  • Better than Delaware: Wyoming's statute is more explicit and has stronger case law supporting single-member protection
üîí Privacy Protections
‚ñº
  • Articles of Organization: Only LLC name, registered agent, and organizer name are public (organizer can be your attorney/service)
  • No member disclosure: Ownership names NOT filed with state‚Äîyour name never appears publicly
  • No manager disclosure: Management structure is private‚Äînot in public records
  • Operating Agreement: Internal document, never filed with state, completely private
  • Annual Report privacy: Only lists registered agent address‚Äîno owner information
  • Registered agent strategy: Use commercial RA service to avoid your home/business address in public records
  • Comparison to California: CA requires Statement of Information with all member/manager names publicly searchable online
  • Privacy limits: Banks know ownership (KYC), IRS knows (K-1s), FinCEN BOI reporting (not public), legal discovery can pierce
  • Best for: Real estate investors avoiding tenant targeting, online businesses protecting from competitors, asset protection strategies
üåê Foreign Qualification: When & Where
‚ñº
  • What triggers it: Physical office, employees, regularly conducting business, owning/leasing property, maintaining inventory
  • What doesn't trigger: Occasional sales, online sales to customers in that state, attending conferences, isolated transactions
  • California threshold: Approximately $757K revenue OR 25% of property/payroll in CA
  • Texas threshold: Doing business in TX‚Äîbroad definition includes regular business activity
  • Cost example: WY LLC + CA foreign qualification = $62/yr (WY) + $800/yr (CA) + $100-300/yr (CA registered agent)
  • When Wyoming makes sense anyway: Asset protection benefit outweighs dual compliance cost (e.g., real estate investor with CA properties)
  • Pure online business: If you have no physical nexus anywhere, Wyoming LLC alone is perfect
  • Penalty for non-compliance: Fines, inability to sue in state courts, back taxes + penalties + interest
üí∞ Cryptocurrency & Digital Assets
‚ñº
  • Legal recognition: Wyoming was first state to recognize digital assets as property with clear ownership rights
  • DAO LLCs: Wyoming allows Decentralized Autonomous Organization LLCs‚Äîblockchain-based governance
  • No state capital gains: 0% Wyoming state tax on crypto gains (federal taxes still apply)
  • Asset protection: Crypto held in Wyoming LLC gets same charging order protection as other assets
  • Privacy advantage: Crypto holdings in LLC name, not personal name‚Äîadds layer of privacy
  • Banking consideration: Some crypto-friendly banks (e.g., Mercury, Relay) work well with Wyoming LLCs
  • Estate planning: Easier to pass crypto to heirs via LLC ownership transfer than private keys
  • Use cases: Bitcoin holdings, Ethereum staking, NFT collections, DeFi protocol governance tokens
üè† Real Estate Investment Strategy
‚ñº
  • One LLC per property: Isolate liability‚Äîlawsuit over Property A can't reach Property B
  • Privacy benefit: Deed shows "Mountain Peak LLC" not "John Smith"‚Äîharder for tenants/plaintiffs to find all your assets
  • Cost efficiency: $62/year per Wyoming LLC vs $800/year per California LLC for CA investors
  • Out-of-state property: Wyoming LLC can own property in any state‚Äîmust foreign qualify in property's state
  • Example structure: CA investor owns 5 rental properties in CA ‚Üí 5 Wyoming LLCs (each foreign qualified in CA) + 1 Wyoming holding LLC
  • Holding company layer: Wyoming Holding LLC owns all property LLCs‚Äîadds another asset protection layer
  • Financing consideration: Some lenders uncomfortable with out-of-state LLCs‚Äîmay need to season title or use portfolio lenders
  • Insurance still required: LLC provides legal protection, but liability insurance is still essential
üìù Operating Agreement Best Practices
‚ñº
  • Wyoming default rules: Very flexible LLC statute, but customize Operating Agreement for your needs
  • Single-member LLCs: Operating Agreement is CRITICAL to prove separate entity status and maintain liability protection
  • Multi-member must-haves: Capital contributions, profit/loss allocation (can differ from ownership %), voting rights, buyout provisions
  • Management structure: Member-managed (all owners manage) vs manager-managed (designated manager)‚Äîimpacts authority
  • Transfer restrictions: Right of first refusal, required member approval, drag-along/tag-along rights
  • Dissolution triggers: What happens if member dies, goes bankrupt, or wants out‚Äîplan ahead
  • Charging order language: Explicitly state charging order as exclusive remedy‚Äîstrengthens asset protection
  • Banking requirement: All banks require Operating Agreement to open business account
  • IRS importance: Multi-member LLCs must file Form 1065‚ÄîOperating Agreement proves legitimacy
  • Annual review: Update as business grows, new members join, or tax law changes
üíº Wyoming for Non-U.S. Residents
‚ñº
  • No citizenship requirement: Anyone globally can form and own a Wyoming LLC
  • EIN process: Without SSN/ITIN, apply via IRS Form SS-4 by fax/mail‚Äîtakes 4-6 weeks
  • Registered agent required: Must hire Wyoming-based registered agent (included in my service)
  • Banking challenges: Most U.S. banks require in-person visit or SSN‚Äîconsider Mercury, Wise, Payoneer for international founders
  • Tax treaty benefits: Check if your country has U.S. tax treaty to reduce withholding on certain income
  • ITIN option: Consider applying for ITIN (Individual Taxpayer ID) for easier banking and credit access
  • U.S. trade or business: If LLC is "engaged in U.S. trade or business," profits taxed federally‚Äîconsult international tax CPA
  • Passive investments: Real estate rentals, stock portfolios generally better tax treatment‚Äî30% withholding vs ordinary rates
  • Common use: International investors use Wyoming LLCs to hold U.S. real estate, Amazon FBA businesses, dropshipping operations
  • Compliance: Must file Form 1065 if multi-member LLC, even with zero activity
üèõÔ∏è BOI Reporting (Beneficial Ownership)
‚ñº
  • January 2025 update: FinCEN exempted most U.S.-formed domestic companies from BOI reporting
  • Typical Wyoming LLC: Owned by U.S. persons currently has NO federal BOI filing obligation
  • Foreign-owned LLCs: More complex‚Äîmay still have reporting requirements depending on structure
  • 23 exemptions: Large operating companies, securities issuers, banks, insurance companies automatically exempt
  • If filing required: Report filed at FinCEN, not with Wyoming‚Äîincludes beneficial owner names, addresses, ID
  • Beneficial owner defined: Anyone with 25%+ ownership or substantial control
  • Not public: BOI reports go to federal database accessible only to law enforcement, banks, and regulators‚Äînot public
  • Stay informed: This area has seen court challenges and policy changes‚Äîconsult attorney for current requirements
